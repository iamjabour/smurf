Over 55,000 of you cast your ballots for the top "Storms of the Century" in December of 1999.

As with any survey, there was some disagreement over which storms should be ranked and in what order.

The Severe Weather Experts at The Weather Channel set up some ground rules to determine which storms should be included in their top ten list.

On the most basic level, to be included on the list a storm had to be just that: a storm. No droughts, heat waves, or cold waves. And it had to be a storm (singular). No multiple events could be combined.

After the top ten was decided upon, a list of Honorable Mentions was compiled to recognize other storms that have impacted the United States between the years 1900 to 1999.
