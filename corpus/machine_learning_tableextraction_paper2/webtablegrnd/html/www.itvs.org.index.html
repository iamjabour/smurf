&lt;body&gt; &lt;h2&gt; ITVS - The Independent Television Service provides funding for production of Public TV films &lt;/h2&gt; Public Television programs by Independent Filmmakers &lt;p&gt; ITVS was established by Congress to fund and present programs that take creative risks, advance issues and represent points of view not usually seen on television. ITVS is committed to programming which addresses the needs of underserved audiences, particularly minorities and children. Granting artistic control to independent filmmakers, ITVS solicits funds, packages, promotes and distributes work for broadcast primarily on American public television. ITVS seeks proposals which increase diversity on public television and present a range of subjects, viewpoints and forms that complement and challenge existing public television offerings. &lt;p&gt; On the ITVS website you can learn more about award winning program offerings such as Forgotten Fires, A Letter Without Words, Kontum Diary: The Journey Home, The Farmers Wife, and Travis. &lt;p&gt; If you are viewing this text, your browser lacks the ability to read frames.
