PREVIEW SYSTEMS REPORTS FOURTH QUARTER AND YEAR END 2000 RESULTS

SUNNYVALE, Calif. ­ February 6, 2001 ­ ), a leading digital commerce platform provider for the secure delivery of digital goods through the Internet, today announced financial results for the fourth quarter and fiscal year ended December 31, 2000. In addition, the company announced it is reducing employee headcount by approximately 25 percent to reduce the company´s cost structure. Parallel to these cost efficiency measures, Preview Systems is in negotiations concerning strategic alternatives to further enhance shareholder value.

Preview Systems reported fourth quarter revenues of $1.85 million, an increase of 52 percent. For fiscal 2000, revenues increased $4.29 million or 123 percent to $7.77 million. Revenues related to network transaction fees for the quarter totaled $1.58 million or 85 percent of total fourth quarter revenues with services accounting for the remaining 15 percent of quarterly revenues.

Including acquisition related charges and stock based compensation costs, the company reported a fourth quarter net loss of $6.8 million, or ($0.40) per share, compared to a loss of $5.5 million or ($0.94) per share in the corresponding 1999 quarter. Net loss for 2000 was $24.5 million or ($1.44) per share compared with a net loss of $17.1 million or ($4.65) per share in 1999. Excluding acquisition related costs and costs associated with stock­based compensation, Preview Systems posted a pro forma fourth quarter loss of $5.6 million or ($0.32) per share compared to a pro forma loss of $4.4 million or ($0.74) per share in the fourth quarter of 1999. The pro forma net loss for 2000 was $17.9 million or ($1.05) per share compared to a pro forma net loss of $14.2 million or ($3.87) per share for 1999.

Preview Systems ended 2000 with cash and investments of $79.5 million or approximately $4.61 per share.

Financial and Operational Highlights For The Fourth Quarter Include:
  • Record quarterly transaction volume in excess of $11.6 million
  • 17 customers went live during the quarter, 70% of our customer base offering products for digital sales
  • Signed Amazon.com as key retailer in indirect channel
  • Signed Flycode and delivered first version of peer­to­peer video solution
  • Signed Matsushita Electric Industrial Co., Ltd. as Ziplock for Music customer in Japan
  • Aqueduct, formerly BuyNow.com, licensed Preview Systems technology for its electronic software distribution services
  • Symantec expanded its use of Preview Systems technology for its international digital storefronts
  • Music distribution platform added support for Convera´s Internet Software Integrity System digital rights management technology, the Windows Media Rights Manager, the Advanced Audio Coding format, and the Secure Digital format
  • Two new patent applications filed, bringing our intellectual property portfolio to 9 issued and allowed and 35 filed patent applications.
  • Strong cash position ­ $79.5 million in cash and investments (approximately $4.61 per share) as of December 31, 2000

Vincent Pluvinage, president and chief executive officer of Preview Systems stated, "While we continue to add important new customers, our flat sequential revenue is an indication that the market for digital downloads, while promising longer term, is taking longer to develop than we had previously anticipated. As a result, we have taken steps to reduce our cost structure through the immediate reduction of 37 positions from a total workforce of 140 people, a headcount reduction of approximately 25 percent. Reflecting the job cuts, among other efficiency initiatives, we will take a charge, amounting to approximately $750,000 in the first quarter to cover the costs of these actions. In addition, due to significant changes in market valuations, the changing competitive landscape and the consolidation within our industry, the company is currently evaluating strategic alternatives to enhance shareholder value. While the negotiations are ongoing, no definitive agreement has been reached concerning sale of the company´s business."

"Despite the aforementioned factors, we continued to make solid progress on many operational fronts during the fourth quarter. Preview Systems posted record transaction volume, signed several new direct customers, and added Matsushita as a direct licensee in Japan as well as Amazon.com to our indirect network of customers. We also made progress on the technology front, adding important features to our solution for secure music distribution, including support for Convera´s Internet Software Integrity System digital rights management technology. If we look at early 2001, Preview Systems remains focused on expanding its digital distribution network with the licensing of our technology platform to NTT Communications, following more than six months of rigorous evaluation and testing," Pluvinage added.

About Preview Systems:

Preview Systems´ digital rights commerce platform enables service providers and content producers to sell digital goods over the Internet legally and securely. Preview Systems also ensures the protection, delivery and management of the rights associated with the usage of digital products. Headquartered in Sunnyvale, Calif., the company counts as its customers a network of software publishers, record labels, distributors, service providers, resellers and hardware manufacturers. For more information, please visit the Preview Systems Web site at www.previewsystems.com .
