The Louisiana Public Service Commission is an independent regulatory agency created in 1921 by the Louisiana Constitution. It succeeded the Railroad Commission of Louisiana created by the 1898 Constitution.

The LPSC consists of five (5) elected members who serve overlapping terms of six (6) years.

The overall mission of the LPSC is to regulate the rates and service practices of utility and transportation companies located in Louisiana in order to secure rates that are fair to the consumer and to the companies.
