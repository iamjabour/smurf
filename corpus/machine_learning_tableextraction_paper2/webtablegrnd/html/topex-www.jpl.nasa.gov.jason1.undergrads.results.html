Science Results

The following are some TOPEX/Poseidon studies. For a more complete overview, check the AVISO Newsletter, April 1998. The newsletter contains summaries of over fifty research areas. The newsletter is available from topex@jpl.nasa.gov .

Global Observations of Rossby Waves Characteristics of low-frequency, large-scale Rossby waves as investigated from 3 years of TOPEX/Poseidon data. Dudley B. Chelton and Michael G. Schlax
Ocean circulation variations associated with the Antarctic Circumpolar Wave Propagation of sea surface height anomalies eastward about the Antarctic continent.

G. A. Jacobs and J. L. Mitchell

Surface currents Analysis of tropical Pacific near-surface currents, based on Ekman and geostrophic dynamics, using high resolution wind and altimeter sea level data. Gary S.E. Lagerloef
Comparison of TOPEX Sea Surface Heights and Tide Gauge Sea Levels TOPEX sea surface height data from the first 300 days of the mission compared to sea level data from 71 tide gauges. Gary T. Mitchum
The Source of 90-day Oscillations at Wake Island Eddies as a source of energetic 90-day oscillations in sea level intermittently observed at Wake Island in the western tropical Pacific during the past two decades. Gary T. Mitchum
Kuroshio Extension Dynamics Sea surface height fluctuations in the Kuroshio Extension and its southern recirculation gyre regions (136E-180, 25N-40N) as seen from TOPEX/Poseidon data for September 1992 - June 1994) Bo Qiu
