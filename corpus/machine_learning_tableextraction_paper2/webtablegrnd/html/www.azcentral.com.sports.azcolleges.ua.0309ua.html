Wildcats score late upset

Richard Obert

The Arizona Republic

March 9, 2001

UA report

THUMBS UP

Wing players Gilbert Arenas and Richard Jefferson played as if they want to win a national title.

THUMBS DOWN

Casey Jacobsen launched a long three-pointer that missed and gave UA a chance to win.

INJURIES

None.

PLAYER OF THE GAME

Michael Wright for his power move in the final seconds that beat the Cardinal.

STANFORD, Calif. - Back and forth they went, the Pac-10's top heavyweights, until forward Michael Wright powered his way between the Collins twins to lift eighth-ranked Arizona past top-ranked Stanford 76-75 on Thursday night at Maples Pavilion.

It was only the second loss this season for Stanford (27-2), both at Maples. UCLA won the other. UA delayed Stanford's outright conference-title, net-cutting celebration.

With his team trailing by one, Wright took the ball inside and used his strength to wedge in for the winning basket with three seconds left.

"Loren (Woods) threw me a great pass and I was able to muscle the ball in," Wright said.

Gilbert Arenas intercepted the inbounds pass and a faction of Arizona fans chanted "U of A" as the Wildcats walked off, avenging January's loss to the Cardinal in Tucson.

This was as billed - a war.

There were 10 lead changes in the second half.

Arenas against Casey Jacobsen. Michael Wright against the Collins twins, Jason and Jarron. Woods against the world.

And in the end, the Wildcats somehow found a way to pull out of a 10-point hole in the first half and a seven-point deficit in the second.

Arenas, who had 22 points, gave UA a 71-69 lead with a steal and a slam, but Stanford scored the next six points, four by center Jason Collins, who was playing with four fouls. Collins had 20 points.

An Arenas basket from the key cut the lead to 75-73 with 1:16 left. Jason Gardner made one of two foul shots with 42 seconds left to make the score 75-74.

Jacobsen, who had 20 points, missed a three-pointer with 15 seconds left and Arizona rebounded, setting up for the last shot.

"We've been through a lot of ups and downs," Wright said. "The chemistry wasn't there early in the Pac-10. We've got it back."

Wildcats forward Richard Jefferson, who had 14 points, said he thinks Stanford will be a Final Four team.

"I think Stanford is a lock for the Final Four," Jefferson said. "We're the ones that need to get there."

Arizona coach Lute Olson said: "That was a great college basketball game, not only for how hard the two teams played, but the fans, right from the start, were into it.

"I told Mike (Montgomery, Stanford coach) there are some games where there shouldn't be a loser. Stanford was not a loser. We're fortunate. Had they won, they would have been fortunate too."

The Wildcats had to scramble in the final six minutes of the first half to get within a basket.

Stanford built a 10-point lead without Jason Collins, who picked up three fouls in the first 41/2 minutes. Collins had given the Cardinal their first eight points, six on a pair of three-pointers.

UA coach Lute Olson had 7-foot center Loren Woods on Collins' twin, Jarron, because he is quicker and puts the ball on the floor better. Wright at power forward was matched up against Jason Collins.

UA's plan early was to attack inside. It worked. Wright took the ball strong to the basket and Jason Collins found himself a seat on the bench because of the fouls.

Teyo Johnson replaced Collins and picked up two fouls in the half.

Still, Stanford burned the Wildcats on the perimeter. Point guard Michael McDonald blew past Jason Gardner a couple times for baskets. Jacobsen and reserve Julius Barnes each hit two three-pointers.

The Cardinal shot 50 percent in the first half, but the Wildcats controlled the boards 24-12 against the Pac-10's best rebounding team.
