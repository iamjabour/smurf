PUMATECH, INC. ANNOUNCES SECOND QUARTER 2000 OPERATING RESULTS

Listen to Second Quarter 2000 operating results conference call.

San Jose, CA (February 24, 2000) - PUMATECH, Inc. (Nasdaq: PUMA) announced the results of its second fiscal quarter of 2000 ended January 31, 2000. Revenue for the second quarter of 2000 increased 48% to $7,030,000 as compared to $4,742,000 reported in the second fiscal quarter of 1999. This quarter&rsquos revenue represents the fifth consecutive quarter of sequential revenue growth as well as record revenue achievement for the Company. Reported diluted loss per share for the second fiscal quarter of 2000 was $(.02) as compared to a diluted loss per share of $(.15) reported in the second quarter of fiscal 1999. Pro-forma diluted earnings per share, excluding amortization of goodwill and intangible assets associated with all previous acquisitions, was $.03 in the second fiscal quarter of 2000 as compared to a pro-forma diluted loss per share of $(.14) in the second quarter of fiscal 1999.

&ldquoFiscal Q2 was a strong quarter for the Company as we experienced continued revenue momentum from our core businesses, expanded our presence in multiple marketplaces and secured important partnerships across multiple product lines,&rdquo said Brad Rowe, CEO of Puma. &ldquoWith the recent acquisitions of NetMind and ProxiNet, the Company is uniquely positioned as the leading provider of essential software infrastructure for the ubiquitous mobile Internet. We hold market-leading positions in synchronization, personalization, notification, and web rendering services&mdashall essential building blocks to make wireless devices work in the world of e-business. We will continue to focus on market opportunities, strategic partnerships and product development which further distances us from the rest of pack.&rdquo

ABOUT PUMATECH

PUMATECH is the leading provider of the essential software infrastructure for the ubiquitous mobile Internet. The Company owns market-leading technology in synchronization, personalization, notification and Web-rendering services &ndash all essential building blocks to making wireless devices useful tools in the world of e-business. Puma's recently acquired NetMind‘ technology delivers highly personalized, relevant Internet content to more than six million active users. In addition, the Company's Intellisync“ platform is the de facto global standard for synchronization and control of the critical connection between handheld devices and mobile phones and the vast stores of information found in corporate databases, the Internet and individual PC's. The Intellisync platform enables mobile access to accurate, "anytime, anywhere" information and has been adopted worldwide by individuals, corporations, mobile device manufacturers, telecommunication carriers, and on-line contact, calendar and event services. The Company, headquartered in San Jose, CA, maintains a website at www.pumatech.com
