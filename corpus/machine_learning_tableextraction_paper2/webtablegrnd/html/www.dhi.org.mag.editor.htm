Calling All Authors!

Publishing an article in the only magazine devoted exclusively to the architectural openings industry establishes you and your company as an authority in your field. Authorship allows you to share ideas, gain peer recognition and enhance communication among industry professionals. We welcome general topic articles, which will be used at the discretion of the editors and as space allows. Articles should not be promotional in nature. However, Case Studies are accepted, which highlight situations/problems handled during a specific installation. Particular products can be discussed as they apply to these specific cases.

As an author, you will be entered as a candidate for the Robert G. Ryan Award. This prestigious award is presented annually to volunteer authors who convey valuable information in a readable and comprehensible format for readers of Doors and Hardware . Awards are given for the best technical articles and for the best business-related or general topic articles.

Your Audience

Articles in Doors and Hardware cover technical subjects, specialty products, codes, changes and growth within the industry, and business management. The magazine serves as the primary resource for emerging issues, developments and timely events that affect its readers. The audience is made up of architects, specifiers, distributors, manufacturers, sales agents, engineers, building owners and managers, contractors, independent consultants and code officials ? all involved in the details of the architectural openings industry. Readers want up-to-date information on technical issues, product management information, general advice on how to improve performance, as well as current trends in the industry.

An article by-line will include your name and company. The ?Contributors? page will include your name, title, company and location (city and state), as well as your picture, if one is available.

Authors should write informative features with educational merit for an audience composed primarily of professional architectural hardware distributors, specifiers, manufacturers and architects. When writing about a subject found on the editorial schedule or when approaching a subject agreed upon between you and the editors, an effort should be made to keep your topic relevant to these professionals. Doors and Hardware readers specify, order, distribute, design, manufacture and consult on doors and architectural hardware.

The objective is to educate and inform our readership; therefore, articles should not be promotional in nature. Advertising by parties who are submitting articles is acceptable but will not be placed within the article. The publications staff will also make every effort to ensure that competitor advertising will not be placed within the article.

Editorial Guidelines

Use the following points as a checklist to communicate effectively:



? Keep the audience in mind and the subject matter relevant.

? Check and review facts carefully.

? Proofread the article for accuracy, logic, spelling and grammar.

? Avoid promotional statements about products or companies.



Feature articles should be approximately 1,200 to 3,000 words (7 to 10 double-spaced, typed pages) in length. Columns are shorter than features and should be from 700 to 1,500 words (4 to 6 pages). Column topics can include, for example:



? ADA Updates - Changes in recommendations

? Sales Edge - Innovative sales techniques that are successful

? Tech Tips - Technical subjects; product use and installation



(A complete list of column topics is available upon request. Contact publications@dhi.org )



Submitting Your Article

1. Final manuscripts should be received by our editorial office by the first weekday of the month, two months prior to publication.

2. Attach a short biography that includes your name, job title, company and location, and any professional credentials. You may also include a black & white or color photograph of yourself for the ?Contributors? page of the magazine.

3. Submit clear, typewritten copy or a computer diskette that is PC compatible, such as an IBM 3-1/2" diskette in Microsoft Word or ASCII text file formats. Number all pages. Articles may also be sent via e-mail to publications@dhi.org .

4. When appropriate, please provide illustrations, technical drawings, tables, charts or graphs to enhance your article. Photographs of products themselves are usually not accepted. Installation photographs are acceptable. If you wish to have your photographs or slides returned to you, label each one ?Return to (your company/name and address).? To send illustrations electronically, send them in EPS or TIF format at 100% in the highest resolution possible ? at least 300 dpi.



Process

As articles are prepared for publication, they are edited for clarity, style and length following the Associated Press Style Book for general editing and ANSI guidelines for technical data. Unsolicited articles are held until magazine space is available, and used only if editors determine they are appropriate for publication.
