Vitesse Reports Results for Second Quarter of Fiscal 2000



Camarillo, CA - (April 17, 2000) - Vitesse Semiconductor Corporation ( NASDAQ:VTSS ) ("Vitesse" or the "Company") today reported results for the second quarter of fiscal 2000 ended March 31, 2000. Revenues in the second quarter of fiscal 2000 were $100.2 million, an increase of 50% over the $66.9 million in the second quarter of fiscal 1999 and an increase of 12% over the $89.2 million in the first quarter of fiscal 2000. For the six month period ended March 31, 2000, revenues were $189.4 million, a 48% increase over the $127.6 million for the six month period ended March 31, 1999.



Pro forma net income for the second quarter of fiscal 2000, which excludes the write-off of purchased in-process R&D ("IPR&D") discussed below, was $27.0 million or $0.16 per share, compared to net income of $15.5 million or $0.09 per share in the second quarter of fiscal 1999 and net income of $23.6 million or $0.14 per share in the prior quarter.

During the second quarter of 2000, the Company acquired Orologic, Inc. ("Orologic") for $450 million in common stock. Orologic, founded in October 1997, is a fabless semiconductor company that develops high performance system-on-a-chip solutions that enable data packet processing at OC-48 and OC-192 rates for ATM and IP traffic. The acquisition, which was completed on March 31, 2000, was recorded under the purchase method of accounting and the fair values of the acquired assets and liabilities were included in the Company's financial statements beginning March 31, 2000. As a result, the Company incurred a one-time charge of $45.6 million or a net loss of $0.29 per share on an after tax basis as a write-off of purchased IPR&D. The Company has recorded goodwill and other identifiable intangibles amounting to approximately $408 million which will be amortized over a period up to six years.



Including the above mentioned write-off of purchased IPR&D, the Company incurred a net loss of $18.6 million or a net loss per share of $0.12 for the quarter ended March 31, 2000, compared to net income of $15.5 million or $0.09 per share in the second quarter of 1999 and net income of $23.6 million or $0.14 per share in the prior quarter. For the six month period ended March 31, 2000, net income was $5.0 million or $0.03 per share which included the write-off of IPR&D, compared to net income of $30.1 million or $0.18 per share in the first half of fiscal 1999.



Vitesse President and CEO, Lou Tomasetta, said "This was another excellent quarter with continued strength in sales of our datacom products and accelerating growth in sales of our telecom products due to increased production volume and shipments of OC-192 systems. On a strategic note, the acquisition of Orologic, with its traffic management and ATM SAR chips, solidified our position as the most complete solution provider for OC-48 network processor applications."

Vitesse Semiconductor Corporation is the leading designer and supplier of innovative, high performance semiconductors utilizing state-of-the-art processes -- for the communications equipment manufacturers of next generation DWDM and optical networking, as well as storage area networking ICs and enclosure management products that monitor and communicate critical reliability and availability information. The company's products address the needs of Core, Access, Optical and Enterprise network equipment manufacturers who demand a combination of high speed, high complexity and low power dissipation. Vitesse is headquartered in Camarillo, California, and manufactures volume ICs at its two fabrication facilities in Camarillo and Colorado Springs.
