Companies Merge CRM Marketing Muscle

Corporate strategy consultant Mercer Management Consulting and Applied Predictive Technologies (APT), a CRM marketing software provider, have announced a strategic alliance to help businesses improve their real-time marketing programs via online channels.

"Long-term success in real-time marketing requires an emphasis on rapid prototyping and a test-and-learn mentality," said Jim Manzi, founder and chief executive officer of APT. "Too many e-commerce businesses have failed in this fast-moving market because they were unable to bring value to their online channels."

Real-Time Marketing Tests

According to the two companies, the agreement will combine the "customer science" capabilities of Mercer's Nexperiment and APT's CRM marketing optimization software. The two applications will work together to let users increase revenues and value from their online channels.

Nexperiment is a patent-pending program that lets companies test, analyze and improve offers, promotions, pricing, sales and support via a test-and-learn approach, Mercer said. By testing a finite number of marketing stimuli with a relatively small sample of customers, Nexperiment helps companies predict the results of their marketing efforts.

APT said its predictive software lets businesses load and test up-sell, cross-sell and retention campaigns in real-time using direct channels.

When the two companies' programs are combined, Mercer's Nexperiment also will work in real-time, and its analysis outcomes will be available via APT's platform, the companies said.

Customize Pitch, Score More

According to the companies, the combination of their offerings will let marketing managers deliver offers that vary by customer and that ensure the highest rate of acceptance.

Mercer vice president Eric Almquist noted that marketing has become more challenging due to a growing glut of goods and services, among other factors.

"Customers are evaluating more value propositions from more companies," he said.

Almquist added that the companies' combined technologies will let businesses profit from their CRM investments by efficiently testing thousands of marketing alternatives.

"If you don't get your offers right, your promotions on target and prices optimized for profit, you will lose the game," he said.
