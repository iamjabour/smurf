JOHN WILEY & SONS, INC.

REPORTS STRONG FISCAL 2000 RESULTS:

EARNINGS PER SHARE UP 35% - REVENUES ADVANCE 17%

New York, N.Y., June 22, 2000 - John Wiley & Sons, Inc., (NYSE:JWa) (NYSE:JWb)announced today that earnings per share for the fiscal year ended April 30, 2000, advanced 35% to $0.81 per diluted share compared with $0.60 per diluted share in the prior year. Net income of $52.4 million increased 32% over the prior year. The company's operating margin reached 15% in fiscal 2000, or 18% excluding the amortization of intangibles.

Revenues for the year increased 17% to $594.8 million compared with $508.4 million in the prior year. Revenue growth was 8% year over year, excluding the current year acquisitions and the foreign exchange translation effects of weaker European currencies.

For the fourth quarter of fiscal 2000, earnings per diluted share increased 30% to $0.13 compared with $0.10 in the prior year period. Net income of $8.2 million for the fourth quarter increased 26% over the same prior year period. For the fourth quarter of fiscal 2000, revenues of $149.1 million were up 20% over the prior year's $124.7 million.

Revenue and income gains for the year reflect improvement in all of the company's businesses. The company continued to gain market share through a combination of organic growth, as well as the successful integration of the acquisitions completed during the current fiscal year. Current year acquisitions exceeded our expectations and were accretive to earnings by $0.03 per diluted share.

The company continued its active share repurchase program during the fourth quarter acquiring over 500,000 shares. During fiscal 2000, the company acquired over 2 million shares at an average price of $15.74.

Segment Highlights

The domestic professional/trade segment registered a 36% increase in revenues over the prior year, led by the current year acquisitions, including Jossey-Bass, a San Francisco-based professional publisher, and the J.K. Lasser tax and financial guides. The increase in revenues was also attributable to the continued growth in sales through online accounts and strong frontlist titles.

Domestic college revenues advanced 30% for the year driven by a strong frontlist and the current year acquisition of approximately 60 titles from Pearson. Domestic scientific, technical and medical (STM) revenues increased 5% for the year.

International results were strong across the board. European operating results reflect healthy gains in STM journals, although revenues were somewhat depressed due to the strong U.S. dollar. The Canadian and Australian businesses delivered strong performances, while Asia's results improved significantly as the economy continued to strengthen.

?The company's performance during this past fiscal year was outstanding, both in terms of our financial results and the strategic initiatives that are driving Web-enabled revenue growth,? said William J. Pesce, President and Chief Executive Officer. ?We achieved a 15% operating margin, one year ahead of plan, while continuing to invest in the future through internal development and acquisitions. The two major acquisitions completed during the year were successfully integrated and delivered results that exceeded our initial expectations.?

He concluded by saying, ?Our results reflect Wiley's determination not to be limited by either/or thinking. Our future is not about digital or print products. It is not about quality or profits. It is not about content or distribution. Our future is about the powerful combination of all these things. We are enthusiastic about our ability to drive Web-enabled revenue growth by making Wiley's deep reservoir of ?must-have? content more easily accessible to our customers, while continuing to deliver strong financial results.?

Business Highlights

During the quarter, the company entered into an agreement with CNBC, a worldwide leader in business news, to publish a series of books that will provide insight into personal investing. In the distance learning arena, the company is working with Caliber Learning Network to provide online courses for the higher education and corporate lifelong learning markets, and with WebCT and Blackboard to offer interactive syllabi, chat rooms and assessment tools, including online quizzing and testing.

In addition, the company entered into a transatlantic alliance with the International Securities Market Association in Switzerland to create Internet-based finance courses, and an alliance with the Institute for Operations Research and Management Science to provide online learning courses. The company also acquired an equity stake in InPharm Internet Services Ltd., an Oxford-based B2B start-up that provides online information resources for the pharmaceutical industry.

Wiley InterScience , the company's Web-based service, continued to expand its offerings to include EarlyView, which allows customers to access individual journal articles online well in advance of the print issue. The online version of the Wiley Encyclopedia of Electrical and Electronic Engineering was recently launched representing the first of approximately 30 major reference works to be added to Wiley InterScience by the end of 2001. During the quarter, customers continued to sign enhanced access licenses on business terms that are attractive to them and to Wiley. CrossRef , the collaborative online reference-linking service supported by 33 publishers, including Wiley, is now live. CrossRef is designed to facilitate the research process by offering researchers the ability to move seamlessly from one article to another.

Founded in 1807, John Wiley & Sons, Inc., is a global publisher of print and electronic products, specializing in scientific, technical, and medical books and journals; professional and consumer books and subscription services; and textbooks and other educational materials for undergraduate and graduate students as well as lifelong learners. The company has publishing, marketing and distribution centers in the United States, Canada, Europe, Asia, and Australia. The company's Class A and Class B shares are listed on the New York Stock Exchange under the symbols JWa and JWb, respectively. The company's Internet site can be accessed at http://www.wiley.com
