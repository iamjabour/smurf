About the ninth-grade proficiency tests:



? The original four ninth-grade proficiency tests were given for the first time in November 1990; ninth-grade science was given for the first time in March 1996. Anyone graduating after September 15, 2000, is required to pass the ninth-grade science test and meet the other requirements in order to receive a diploma.



? In 1995, the State Board of Education established an increase in ninth-grade science for the class of 2004 and beyond. Senate Bill 55 phases out the ninth-grade proficiency tests. In November 1998, the State Board rescinded that ninth-grade science increase since it would affect one class of students (the class of 2004) only. Members of the class of 2005

are the first class required to pass new high school graduation tests.



? The last time that eighth graders could take the ninth-grade proficiency tests in the spring was in March 2000; eighth graders in March 2001 are members of the class of 2005.

? The last administration of the ninth-grade proficiency tests will be in Summer 2004. Anyone graduating after September 15, 2004, is required

by Senate Bill 55 to pass new high school graduation qualifying exams.
