NYSE Member Firms Report 1996 Results



New York Stock Exchange member firms that do business with the public reported an after-tax profit of $1.7 billion on record revenues of $31.4 billion in fourth quarter 1996, compared with $1.6 billion and $27.6 billion, respectively, in fourth quarter 1995. In third quarter 1996, the firms reported an after-tax profit of $1.3 billion on revenues of $28.8 billion. In the full year 1996, the firms reported an after-tax profit of $7.3 billion on revenues of $120.3 billion, both records, compared with $4.8 billion and $96.3 billion, respectively, in the full year of 1995.

In fourth quarter 1996, NYSE specialists reported an after-tax profit of $52 million on revenues of $214 million, compared with $36 million and $174 million in the same period of 1995. In third quarter 1996, NYSE specialists reported an after-tax profit of $36 million on revenues of $173 million. In the full year of 1996, specialists reported a record after-tax profit of $201 million on revenues of a record $808 million, compared

with $162 million and $702 million, respectively, for the full year of 1995.

Note: For member firms dealing with the public, the figures collected by the NYSE include revenue from all sources, including trading, interest and commission income. Since the NYSE member firm community is made up of corporations and partnerships, after-tax earnings are presented on a pro-forma basis assuming a regular corporate tax rate or credit of 35 percent.

The category of member firms dealing with the public generally excludes specialists, but includes firms that primarily trade for their own accounts, rather than doing a public business.

After-tax earnings for NYSE specialist units are based on an aggregate tax rate of 44 percent for federal and local taxes.
