Current status of the project to factor numbers of the form b^n +/- 1, b = 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 10, 11, and 12 maintained by Samuel S. Wagstaff.

Tables of known factorizations of the first 10,000 Fibonacci and Lucas numbers.

Andy Steward's research into the form (b^n-1)/(b-1).
Factor Tables - http://web.comlab.ox.ac.uk/oucl/work/richard.brent/factors.html

Richard Brent's tables extend the scope of the Cunningham project (factoring b^n+-1) to all applicable bases b less than 100, plus some higher bases.
Cullen and Woodall numbers - http://research.microsoft.com/~pleyland/factorization/cullen_woodall/cw.htm

Cullen numbers are of the form n.2^n + 1 and Woodall numbers are n.2^n - 1. These tables of Cullen and Woodall numbers include factorizations for n up to 1000.
Smarandache Factors - http://www.sci.kun.nl/sigma/Persoonlijk/michaf/ecm/

Micha Fleuren's summary of the state of factorization of Smarandache and Reverse Smarandache numbers.
Factorization Results - http://www.asahi-net.or.jp/~KC2H-MSM/mathland/matha1/index.htm

Tables, summaries, and links to many factoring endeavors, including primorials, factorials, and cyclotomic polynomials. Includes theorems and descriptions behind each project.
Factorization of Cyclotomic Numbers - http://www.asahi-net.or.jp/~KC2H-MSM/cn/

Hisanori Mishima is coordinating the search and producing tables of factorizations of cyclotomic numbers for values of phi(n) less than 48.
Factors of 2^n + 1 - http://www.euronet.nl/users/bota/medium-p.htm

Factorizations for prime values of n up to 5000, extending the results of the Cunningham Project.
Factors - http://factorzone.tripod.com/factors.htm

Lists the factors of every single number up to 600, for quick reference and as a resource for students.
Prime Factorization of Cyclotomic Numbers - http://science.icu.ac.jp/~morimoto/enbunsu/table.html

Professor Mitsuo Morimoto extends the factor tables of cyclotomic numbers for values of phi(n) up to 120.
Fermat Factoring Status - http://vamri.xray.ufl.edu/proths/fermat.html

Compiled by Wilfrid Keller, lists the known prime factors and complete factorizations of Fermat numbers.
