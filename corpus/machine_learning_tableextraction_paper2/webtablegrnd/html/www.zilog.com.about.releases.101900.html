ZiLOG ANNOUNCES THIRD QUARTER 2000 RESULTS

Net Sales Increase to $66.5 million

Campbell, CA (October 19, 2000) -- ZiLOG Inc. (formerly ZLG on NYSE), the Extreme Connectivity company, today reported net sales of $66.5 million for the third quarter of 2000, as compared to the $64.5 million in revenues during the third quarter of 1999.

"Our net sales reached their highest level in three years," said Curtis J. Crawford, Chairman, President and CEO of ZiLOG. "Our acquisition of wireless connectivity leader, Calibre Inc., during the quarter reinforces our focus on communications products, brings us a solid customer base in the fast growing PDA market, and positions us well in wireless connectivity."

During the quarter, ZiLOG incurred a $1.5 million special charge for in-process research and development expense in connection with the acquisition of Calibre Inc. on July 27, 2000. The company also incurred approximately $1.1 million of IPO-related costs, included in selling, general and administrative expenses. ZiLOG filed a registration statement with the Securities and Exchange Commission for its initial public offering in August 2000 and withdrew the registration statement in September 2000 because of market conditions.

Gross margin for the third quarter of 2000 was 38 percent of sales, compared to 39 percent in the third quarter of 1999. ZiLOG reported operating income of $0.1 million during the third quarter of 2000, compared to $1.8 million operating income reported during the third quarter of 1999.

The company ended the third quarter of 2000 with $35.3 million in cash and cash equivalents. ZiLOG also reported $12.1 million of EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization of intangible assets, equity in loss of Qualcore, non-cash stock compensation expenses and special charges) for the third quarter of 2000, compared with $12.6 million reported for the third quarter of 1999.

Research and development expenses were $9.0 million in the third quarter of 2000, up 6 percent from the same period in 1999, primarily as a result of higher investments in Communications product development.

About ZiLOG Inc.

ZiLOG, (formerly ZLG on the NYSE) the Extreme Connectivity Company, designs, manufactures and markets semiconductor micro-logic devices for the growing communications and embedded control markets. Headquartered in Campbell, Calif., ZiLOG employs approximately 1,300 people worldwide. ZiLOG maintains design centers in Campbell; Austin, Texas; Ft. Worth, Texas; Nampa, Idaho; Seattle, Wash.; and Bangalore, India, a worldwide customer service center in Austin, advanced manufacturing in Nampa and test operations in Manila, Philippines. www.zilog.com
