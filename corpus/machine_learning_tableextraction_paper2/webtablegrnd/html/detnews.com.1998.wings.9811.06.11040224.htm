Notebook: New rules haven't helped Kozlov score

By Ted Kulfan / The Detroit News



DETROIT -- Vyacheslav Kozlov was excited when he first heard about the new rules designed to help skilled players and create more offense in the NHL. A speedster with the ability to find holes and skate through them, Kozlov and the rule changes seemed like a perfect marriage.

So far, though, Kozlov hasn't seen much difference from last season. A lot of interference still exists, and small, quick skaters have difficulty getting around it.

That's part of the problem. On a larger scope, Kozlov has only three points and is looking for his first goal through the opening 10 games.

"It's disappointing," Kozlov said. "They expect goals from me. I should score goals. It's part of the job."

Kozlov is getting opportunities. He's third on the team with 31 shots, and more than a few have been quality chances.

But that elusive first goal has been out of his grasp.

"Getting that first one (goal) will take some pressure off," Kozlov said. "You can play with more freedom and not think about it every day. After that, you can just go play."

Historically, Kozlov has been a player who scores in bunches. For that reason, and the fact the rest of his game has been solid, the Red Wings aren't concerned with the lack of scoring.

"Slava is getting his chances, and they'll start going in for him," associate coach Barry Smith said. "We're not concerned at all. He needs to get that first goal and he'll be fine."

Kozlov said he isn't pressing. There isn't self-inflicted pressure to end the slump. The Red Wings have enough depth to overcome one slow start.

Igor Larionov, a strong veteran influence on Kozlov, has reassured his younger teammate.

"We've talked a couple times and Igor has said to stay patient and keep playing hard, the goals will come," Kozlov said. "I know that is true."

Kozlov also said that the league hasn't opened up the game as it promised.

Players are still clutching and holding. And moving the net out two feet from the backboards (to 13 feet), hasn't produced more offense -- yet.

"It could," Kozlov said. "It might take some time. Teams are playing very aggressively, especially against a team like ours that won the Stanley Cup. It's hard for everyone to score."



Ice chips



One player who made it off the scoreless list was Kirk Maltby, who had two goals in the recent three-game trip.

"On this team we all have roles," said Maltby, whose main responsibility is to prevent goals rather than score them. "I can't lose focus of that. But, sure, when you score it's nice."

... Uwe Krupp (quadriceps) skated Tuesday for the first time since suffering the injury Oct. 23. "Uwe's coming along, it's a gradual thing," Smith said. "We'll take it day to day and see how he feels."

... Joe Kocur (right hand) was a scratch Tuesday. He was replaced by Stacy Roest.
