Indications of Interest. Indications of interest (IOIs) enable the sell side and buy side to communicate trades on the BLOOMBERG PROFESSIONAL® service.

IOIs are single line messages sent from broker/dealers to buy-side institutions, which express their desire to buy or sell blocks of certain stocks. When an institution sees an IOI it would like to act on, it phones the broker to execute the trade. Bloomberg users can have access to IOIs for no additional charge.

IOIs come in two categories: present-tense IOIs and advertised trades. A present-tense IOI expresses the current desire to trade and is sent only to the buy side. Advertised trades are messages announcing that a broker has already executed a trade. They are sent to all users, including other brokers.

Why use IOIs?

To help salespeople do more trades by getting their merchandise in front of clients and prospects more cheaply and easily

To help position traders and market makers market the liquidity they represent and build their reputations

To help buy side traders achieve the best execution to ultimately improve their fund's overall performance

Where does the data come from? All indications of interest come from participating sell-side broker/dealers who feed the indications into the BLOOMBERG PROFESSIONAL® service.
