The Arizona National Stock Show is Arizona's largest livestock show. The 2001 Show awarded over $70,000 is premiums to Junior as well as open exhibitors from 27 states and Canada with over 2100 head of the nations best cattle, horses, sheep and swine competing. Our Junior Market Auction offers 150 of the highest placing market animals for sale, giving these Junior Exhibitors an opportunity to recover their investment and hopefully tuck away some profit for their future.

Grandstand Attractions What has become one of Arizona's largest High School Rodeos will be returning for the third year along with other favorite grandstand events like our own Ranch Rodeo where five member teams of Arizona Ranch Cowboys and Cowgirls show what it really takes to be a working ranch cowboy. The Arizona Working Ranch Horse contest has also become a favorite event where fifteen head of Arizona's best ranch horses showing off their skills, hoping to be named Grand Champion.

See the Cowboy Classics Western Art and Gear Show which showcases the rich and unique life of the American Cowboy through artwork, items of working gear and original music and poetry. Nationally recognized western artists will display their paintings, drawings, bronze sculptures, leather work, spurs, bits, jewlery and even some very unique furniture. See some of the finest Cowboy Poets and Musicians perform throughout the week long Show.

Visit the Farm Experience with fun filled interactive and educational programs for kids such as our special Li'l Buckaroo Rodeo, Cook's Racing Pigs, Farmer for A Day activities, Grocery Store Game, Petting Zoo, pedal tractor races, corn grinding and even a rope making machine. Bring the whole family and sign up your little ones to ride in our Li'l Buckaroo Rodeo and join in on the fun. The Farm Experience is supported by Arizona's Agricultural Commodity organizations including the Arizona Beef Council, Arizona Pork Producers, Arizona Wool Producers, Arizona Nursery Association, United Dairymen of Arizona and the Arizona Department of Agriculture.

Volunteers are always needed and welcome to help with various aspects of producing the Show. If you are interested in volunteering for any of several positions, please contact the Arizona National Office.
