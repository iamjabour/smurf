US consumer prices rise less than expected in April

Washington, May 16 (BridgeNews) - Higher energy prices pushed the consumer price index up 0.3% in April, but the increase was less than expected, reassuring investors that inflation is under control. Excluding volatile food and energy costs, which economists often strip out to get a better sense of the underlying inflation rate, "core" consumer prices rose 0.2%.
