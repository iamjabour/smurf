Global Population Distribution Database

Results and Discussion

The 1990 global population dataset contains 12,857 records and covers 209 countries and 11,783 grid cells about 1.8% of the total 64,800 grid cells worldwide. The population of the countries in the database is 5.291 billion, within 0.07% of the FAO's total estimated world population for 1990, of 5,295 billion (ref. 6). The global population distribution for 1990 with 1° x 1° longitude/latitude resolution is shown in Figure 1 - Gif format (23K) .

Ranking of the Data by Country

Population data has been evaluated according to the amount of census data available for each country, relative to the country size. If we can allocate both the urban and rural population data within a region smaller than or comparable to the cell size (small country, or county in a large country, for example), then the dataset produced will be ranked as "best". If the area where the rural population is distributed is quite large compared to the cell size, but this rural population is small in comparison to the city population, then the dataset produced will be ranked as "good". If the area where the rural population is distributed is quite large compared to the cell size, and this rural population is large in comparison to the city population, then the dataset produced will be treated as "poor". Table 4 lists alphabetically the countries with the best population dataset, while Table 5 shows the countries with good datasets. The data for the countries in Table 6 are considered poor, and must be updated as soon as the better data become available.
