Bonds Trading Overtakes Equity Transactions

Panafrican News Agency (Dakar)

February 13, 2001

> Posted to the web February 13, 2001

Lewis Mwanangombe

> Lusaka, Zambia

Retail trading in government bonds flared on the Lusaka Stock Exchange Monday as activity on the equity market slumped to a trickle of a handful of shares.

Total value transacted in trades spread across the 12- 18- and 24-month bonds stood at 1.37 billion kwacha, pushing up the total market sales to 1.42 billion kwacha. (3,500 kwacha= 1USD).

The 12-month bond recorded the highest sales at 624.59 million kwacha, trading at 99.14 and a yield to maturity of 32.87 percent.

Bonds valued at 276.28 million were transacted in the 18- month category on a yield to maturity of 21.68 percent.

Another 18-month set of issue was valued at 200 million kwacha and a yield to maturity of 18.92 percent.

The value traded in the 24-month bond was one million kwacha with the bonds trading at 100.17 kwacha giving a yield to maturity of 37.10 percent.

In addition, the Lusaka Stock Exchange recorded seven repo-transactions involving 904 million kwacha worth of bonds in the various bond issues.

On the equity market, the volume of trade was significantly reduced with only three companies recording sales and purchases of very reduced shares.

Zambian Breweries Plc recorded the highest number of trades at 10,072 shares in 10 trades, while Standard Chartered Bank and Rothmans Zambia Limited recorded one trade each of 600 and 2,278 shares respectively.
