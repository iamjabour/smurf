Devils quickly score a record

Bruins fall; Hurricanes make playoffs

April 7, 2001

FREE PRESS NEWS SERVICES

EAST RUTHERFORD, N.J. -- John Madden and Patrik Elias scored goals a team-record eight seconds apart Friday night, and the New Jersey Devils ended the Boston Bruins' playoffs hopes with a 5-2 victory

The Bruins' loss and Carolina's 3-2 win over Atlanta combined to clinch a playoff berth for the Hurricanes. Toronto beat Chicago, 1-0, to clinch the other final playoff berth.

"It's incredibly frustrating," Bruins forward and former Devil Brian Rolston said. "We all want to be in the playoffs. It's very disheartening."

Jason Arnott and Petr Sykora also scored, and Elias added his second of the game and 40th of the season later in the third period as the Devils won for the 18th time in their past 20 games and remained two points ahead of Ottawa in the race for the top spot in the Eastern Conference. Martin Brodeur made 14 saves for his 41st win of the season.

Joe Thornton and Mikko Eloranta scored for the Bruins, who lost for only the third time in 11 games.

Carolina 3, Atlanta 2: Martin Gelinas scored a power-play goal with 4:51 left as host Carolina clinched a playoff berth for the second time in three seasons. The win moved the Hurricanes two points ahead of Boston for the eighth and final playoff spot in the Eastern Conference with one game left, against Pittsburgh on Sunday. Atlanta closed its second season with 60 points -- 21 more than its expansion year.

Ottawa 4, N.Y. Islanders 3: Mike Fisher scored the game-winning goal at 3:03 of the third period for the visiting Senators. It was his seventh game-winning goal. The Senators remain two points behind New Jersey in the race for first place in the Eastern Conference. Each team has one game remaining.

Buffalo 2, Washington 1: Dominik Hasek stopped 41 shots as the host Sabres regained sole possession of fourth place in the Eastern Conference. Dave Andreychuk scored the game-winning goal with 6:45 left in regulation, and Miroslav Satan also scored. The Sabres moved two points ahead of idle Philadelphia in the race for home-ice advantage in the first round.

Toronto 1, Chicago 0: Curtis Joseph made 27 saves for his sixth shutout of the season as the visiting Maple Leafs clinched a playoff berth. Steve Thomas scored in the second period.

Minnesota 3, Columbus 2: Darby Hendrickson scored two goals, including the game-winner with 1:20 left, for the host Wild.

Notebook: Washington's Chris Simon was suspended for two games by the NHL for elbowing Florida's Anders Eriksson in Thursday's game.
