NYSE Member Firms Report Second-Quarter Results



NEW YORK, August 28 -- New York Stock Exchange member firms that do business with the public reported an after-tax profit of $1.9 billion on revenues of $33.9 billion in second quarter 1997, compared with $2.3 billion, and $30.6 billion, respectively, in second quarter 1996. In first quarter 1997, the firms reported an after-tax profit of $2.0 billion on revenues of $33.3 billion. In the first six months of 1997, the firms reported an after-tax profit of $3.9 billion on revenues of $67.2 billion, compared with $4.3 billion, and $60.1 billion, respectively, in the first six months of 1996.

In second quarter 1997, NYSE specialists reported an after-tax profit of $70 million on revenues of $258 million, compared with $44 million and $189 million in the same period of 1996. In first quarter 1997, NYSE specialists reported an after-tax profit of $58 million on revenues of $225 million. In the first six months of 1997, specialists reported an after-tax profit of $128 million on revenues of $483 million, compared with $112 million

and $421 million, respectively, in the first six months of 1996.

Note: For member firms dealing with the public, the figures collected by the NYSE include revenue from all sources, including trading, interest and commission income. Since the NYSE member firm community is made up of corporations and partnerships, after-tax earnings are presented on a pro-forma basis assuming a regular corporate tax rate or credit of 35 percent.

The category of member firms dealing with the public generally excludes specialists, but includes firms that primarily trade for their own accounts, rather than doing a public business.

After-tax earnings for NYSE specialist units are based on an aggregate tax rate of 44 percent for federal and local taxes.
