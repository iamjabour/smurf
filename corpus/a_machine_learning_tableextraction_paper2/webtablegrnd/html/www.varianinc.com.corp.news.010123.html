Varian, Inc., Posts Strong First Quarter Results

for Fiscal Year 2001

Earnings Report & Financial Tables (pdf)



Local currency revenue up 26%; EPS up 42%

PALO ALTO, Calif. -- Varian, Inc. (Nasdaq: VARI) today reported financial results which included strong growth in sales and operating profits for the first quarter of fiscal 2001, ended December 29, 2000, compared to the first quarter of fiscal 2000.

Sales for the first quarter of fiscal 2001 were up 26% in local currency revenues. The term "local currency revenues" means sales adjusted for the effects of foreign currency fluctuations. Including currency effects, reported sales grew 20% to $191 million.

Operating earnings for the first quarter of 2001 grew 44% to $21.4 million, up from $14.9 million in the first quarter of fiscal 2000. First quarter net earnings were $12.8 million, or $0.37 diluted earnings per share, compared to $8.5 million or $0.26 diluted earnings per share in the fiscal 2000 period.

Results by Segment

Scientific Instruments revenues rose 22% in local currencies. Including currency effects, reported sales grew 13% to $107 million for the first quarter of 2001. The growth was driven primarily by increased sales of life science products and by targeted industrial applications such as natural gas analysis. Operating margins were 11.2% for the quarter.

Vacuum Technologies local currency revenues climbed 38%. Including currency effects, reported sales grew 31% to $41 million for the first quarter of fiscal 2001. Operating margins were 22.0% for the quarter.

Electronics Manufacturing revenues grew 27% to $43 million in the first quarter of fiscal 2001. A slowdown in demand from some telecommunications customers was offset by the addition of new customers and the acquisition in late October 2000 of the Imagine Manufacturing Solutions operation. Operating margins of 6.4% for the quarter were reduced by the Imagine acquisition and the start-up costs of new customers.

Outlook

"The company's strategy of focusing on expanding markets is delivering solid growth across our product lines, as evidenced by another consecutive quarter of strong results," said Allen J. Lauer, president and chief executive officer. "Most of the industries that we serve continue to remain healthy. But should economic conditions weaken further, Varian's array of products addressing multiple markets should help soften an adverse impact. Varian provides one of the broadest ranges of scientific instruments and vacuum products supporting diverse applications in equally diverse industries around the globe."

"Varian, Inc. will be holding a conference call with securities analysts and investors on January 23, 2001 at 8:00 am Pacific Time. Interested investors are invited to listen to the call by going to www.varianinc.com and clicking the Investors button at the top of the page.

Varian, Inc. (Nasdaq: VARI) is a major supplier of scientific instruments, vacuum technologies, and specialized contract electronics manufacturing services. These businesses serve life science, health care, chemical analysis, industrial, and electronics customers worldwide. The company manufactures in 14 locations in North America, Europe, and the Pacific Rim and employs some 4,000 people. Varian, Inc. had fiscal year 2000 sales of $704 million. Additional information about Varian, Inc. is available at www.varianinc.com
