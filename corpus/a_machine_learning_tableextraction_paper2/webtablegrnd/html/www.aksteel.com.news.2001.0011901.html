AK STEEL REPORTS FOURTH QUARTER AND FULL YEAR 2000 RESULTS


MIDDLETOWN, OH, January 19, 2001 -- AK Steel (NYSE: AKS) today said the company's fourth quarter 2000 earnings were $15.5 million, or $0.14 per diluted share of common stock. Revenues for the fourth quarter were $1,035.2 million on steel shipments of 1,595,000 tons. Operating profit for the quarter was $46.3 million, or $29 per ton shipped. Value-added shipments for the quarter were 89% of total shipments.

For the full year 2000, AK Steel earned $132.4 million, or $1.20 per diluted share of common stock. Operating profit for the year was $338.2 million, or $52 per ton shipped. 2000 revenues were $4,517.2 million on steel shipments of 6,493,000 tons, compared to 1999 revenues and shipments of $4,284.8 million and 6,541,000 tons, respectively. The 1999 shipments included approximately 200,000 tons from a former Dover, Ohio commodity galvanizing line shut down by AK Steel in January of 2000. Value-added products represented 90% of total shipments for the year. Despite soaring energy costs and spot market selling prices that plummeted, especially during the fourth quarter, AK Steel recorded its seventh consecutive year of industry-leading profitability per ton.

"Under the most brutal business conditions in more than two decades, AK Steel's employees again demonstrated what it takes to be globally competitive in the steel industry," said Richard M. Wardrop, Jr., chairman and chief executive officer. "What AK Steel employees have accomplished over the past 28 quarters is unparalleled in the industry," he said.

2000 Accomplishments Include Numerous Safety, Quality and Productivity Records

Topping the company's 2000 highlights were continued improvements in safety performance where total OSHA recordable injuries fell for the eighth consecutive year, to a rate of 1.44, about five times better than the steel industry average. AK Steel continued to garner prestigious customer quality awards, adding in 2000 its second consecutive GM Supplier of the Year award, the DaimlerChrysler quality award, its sixth dual quality and delivery awards from Toyota as well as perfect audit scores in March and September at its specialty steel plants for maintaining QS-9000 quality certification.

AK Steel said productivity records were established in 2000 at every carbon and specialty plant. Notably, the company's Coshocton Works shipped more than 100,000 tons for the first time. In addition, the company's Mansfield Works established new annual production records in melting, casting and hot rolling with salaried employees and a temporary hourly workforce of about 250 employees.

The company's Zanesville and Butler Works helped AK Steel ship record electrical steel tonnage in 2000. The company's Middletown cold mill broke its own world record by rolling 3.35 million tons, and became the first cold mill in the world to roll more than 1 billion linear feet of steel in a year. The mill ultimately rolled more than 191,000 miles of steel in 2000. The company's Rockport Works produced more than 875,000 tons from its Rockport Works continuous galvanizing line. AK Steel's Butler Works successfully converted a final pass stainless anneal and pickle line from nitric acid to an environmentally friendly hydrogen peroxide process, thought to be the first such conversion in the world. The company's Sawhill Tubular Division also shipped record carbon and specialty pipe and tube products.

Dividend Declared on Preferred Shares, Reduced on Common Shares

The company said its board of directors declared a regular quarterly dividend of $0.90625 per share on its Series B $3.625 Cumulative Convertible Preferred Stock, payable March 30, 2001 to shareholders of record March 2, 2001. The company also said in light of current steel market conditions, its board of directors declared a reduced quarterly common stock dividend of $0.0625 per share, payable on February 28, 2001 to shareholders of record on February 1, 2001. "While we regret the need to take this action, we believe this and other cash conservation efforts are prudent steps in light of current business conditions," said Mr. Wardrop.

AK Steel produces flat-rolled carbon, stainless and electrical steel products for automotive, appliance, construction and manufacturing markets, as well as standard pipe and tubular steel products. AK Steel is headquartered in Middletown, Ohio. It employs about 11,000 men and women in plants and offices in Middletown, Coshocton, Mansfield, Warren and Zanesville, Ohio; Ashland, Kentucky; Rockport, Indiana; and Butler, Sharon and Wheatland, Pennsylvania. AK Steel also produces snow and ice control products, and operates a major industrial park on the Houston, Texas ship channel. Additional information about AK Steel is available on the company's web site at www.aksteel.com.
