The News (Lagos)

April 9, 2001

> Posted to the web April 5, 2001

Okafor Ofiebor

> Port Harcourt

Opponents range against the Rivers State governor

The endorsement of the Rivers State Governor, Dr. Peter Odili and President Olusegun Obasanjo for a second term of office by the Rivers State congress of the People's Democratic Party (PDP) in Port Harcourt on Saturday 3 March, seems to be the alibi needed by Odili's political opponents to put him on the firing line.

In what looked like an innocent way of acknowledging what he perceived as wonderful performance of Dr. Peter Odili's administration in office, Chief Monday Ngbor, an Ogoni from Tai Local Government Area, and a former political opponent of Odili, moved the motion for adoption. Mr. Okorie Asita, chairman of the party in Ahoada West Local Government, supported him. As soon as Asita sat down, a deafening ovation of "yes" rent the air.

The state chairman of the PDP, Prince Uche Secondus, in his speech stated that in less than two years, Dr. Peter Odili has embarked on numerous developmental projects and improved the people's standard of living. He called on all members of the party to support and co-operate with the government because in doing so, they would be defending the party, the government and democracy.

Odili who could not betray his shock and embarrassment at the turn of events at the congress reacted thus: "You challenged me, you nearly threw me off my seat when you moved that motion. I thought you would have waited long enough for me to go far enough in the job you have given us." He went further to assert that, "So what we are going to regard your motion to be is a call to greater work, greater work and greater work. More action, more action and more action, until Rivers State is liberated," Odili emphasized.

After the PDP state congress, Chief Chibudom Nwuche, Deputy Speaker of the House of Representatives called a handful of media representatives (excluding TheNEWS), and launched an attack on the state executive members of the party for what he alleged was a sham and mere manipulation by Odili to further his ambition to run for a second term. He warned of the dire consequences of endorsing the outcome of the congress.

However, some ardent supporters of Odili argued that the governor has not indicated his interest in a second term. It would be recalled that during the monthly chat with media executives in the Television programme, "30 minutes in Brick House," the governor denied ever nursing the ambition. He said that he was more preoccupied with fulfilling his campaign promises, thereby increasing his score card than a second term.

For Dr. Marshall Harry, the former chairman of the PDP in the state and national vice-chairman, South-South of the party who worked for Odili's eventual victory at the polls, his relationship with Odili has been anything but cordial. He has been the leading opposition to Odili's administration. His quarrel with Odili is evident in his statement that: "Do you know that since this government came into being, I have not got a job of even N5 million, not even my associate," he told National Interest of 19 March.

"Not even one person from my camp. As a man who founded this party in this state, I have not been given an appointment... What has Odili done for Rivers State? I advised him to open a bank to empower Rivers people". Dr. Harry further alleged that Odili asked him to nominate some people who assisted the party during election to get contract to construct buildings.

"Till today, all those people that he gave the housing units in my local government he refused to pay them because they were recommended by me."

He has, therefore, vowed that Dr. Odili will not come back as governor after 2003. As it were, the battle line has been drawn. But Odili has denied any personal quarrel with Harry. He said though, there were people who expected him to take orders from them and by extension, surrender the mandate of Rivers people to them, but he refused.

The latest of Odili's attackers is one Chief Ambrose Owuru, of Ikwerre extraction, and a Port Harcourt-based legal practitioner. He has unfolded his plans to defeat Odili under the platform of APP in 2003. He has condemned the N13.5 billion gas turbine projects for which Odili's administration is being praised by the people of the state, hoping that it will permanently put a stop to the erratic power supply from NEPA. Owuru is reported to have described it as waste of funds, especially as he is alleged to have said that the power will be enjoyed mostly by non-indigenes". He feels that the real Rivers people did not need gas turbines electricity when NEPA exists. He equally condemned Odili's free school bus system that conveys school children to and from school. His argument is that it is better to give buses to individual schools and concentrate on repairing dilapidated ones.

Watchers of Owuru's criticisms of the above polices regard it as myopic, sentimental and anti-non-indigenes in Rivers State. They question how Owuru could criticize a policy that would benefit inhabitants of a very cosmopolitan Rivers State. "When he becomes governor, will he chase out non-indigenes whose call of duty bring them to the state?" an Odili supporter asked.

Observer however argue that with the intimidating credentials Odili already has gathered for the past 21 months, throwing him away from the Brick House would be a herculean task if he decides to come back. Another reason for the attacks by those eyeing the Brick House is the increase in the revenue allocation to the state. This is in addition to the 13 per cent that is also accruable from gas resources.

His achievements which include being the first to pay N7,500 minimum wage, construction of network of roads, enhanced rural electrification of over 70 communities, provision of traffic light at strategic junctions in Port Harcourt, seem to intimidate his opponents.

With the projection of having a millennium centre, state refinery, new Government House, state-owned propeller Helicopter, new oil palm estates among others, it is going to be a tall dream for those who want to kick out Odili if he decides to run for the second term.

The party hierarchy has waded in and explained that party primaries will still be conducted to choose the state flag bearer for governorship race when the time comes. The endorsement so to speak, does not foreclose the chances of would be aspirants for the post. Odili, however, maintains that the second term is still far away. "I'm more interested in doing more for my people. Second term like the future, is in the hands of the Almighty God".
