Figure Out Your Choices

Part 1 of the Home Building Series

Welcome to the Construction Industry site where we examine ways to improve our own projects and strive to raise the expectations of the entire Construction Industry. Also, be sure to visit all of my Previous Features .

Have You Ever Thought About building YOUR own house . If you are like me you probably have at some point in your life -- you may be even thinking about doing it now. For this reason, I am starting a new series on How to Build your own house. For the next several weeks I am going to touch on the many decisions that you need to make before and during your home building process.

One of the very first things that you need to do is to visit dozens of different types of homes - one story, two story, ranches, colonials, Victorian, contemporary, open design plans, traditional plans, houses on lakes, houses in subdivisions, houses in the country, houses on tree-lined streets and houses in the city. Look at all of the different types of homes that have been built in the city or town that you plan to live in.

This is important for the following reasons. First, you need to find out what styles the "locals" have used to determine what materials and labor skills are available in your area. Second, unless you plan on living in the house forever, you need to find out what your competition will be when it comes time to sell your house. Third, by going to a variety of homes you learn what features you and your family would like to have in a house.

Do not underestimate this time to "look around." This is time well spent and may save you the embarrassment of building a house that you do not like and worse forcing you to live in a house because it is not a marketable sale item.

Therefore, call up an agent to go look for houses in your price range (more to come on this in the future). Be honest with the agent tell him or her that you are thinking about building your own house and want to explore houses in the area. A good agent may even find you a home that is exactly what you want and save you the time and effort of building your home (but, it may also rob you of all of the fun that you are about to have).

We will continue the series next week.
