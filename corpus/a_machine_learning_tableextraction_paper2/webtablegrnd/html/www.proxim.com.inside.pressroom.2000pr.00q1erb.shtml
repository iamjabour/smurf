Proxim, Inc. Reports First Quarter 2000 Results

SUNNYVALE, Calif., April 18, 2000

? Proxim, Inc. (NASDAQ: PROX) announced today its financial results for the first quarter ended March 31, 2000. Revenue for the first quarter of 2000 was $20,051,000 compared to revenue of $15,119,000 for the first quarter of 1999.

During the first quarter of 2000, the company acquired Micrilor, Inc., in a transaction which was accounted for as a pooling of interests, and all historical financial information contained herein has been revised to reflect the acquisition. The results for the first quarter of 2000 include $217,000 of acquisition costs. Micrilor operations generated no revenue or cost of revenue in the first quarter of 2000, and the operating expenses are included in research and development expense. The results for the first quarter of 1999 have been revised to include $369,000 of revenue, $230,000 of cost of revenue, and $124,000 of research and development expenses from Micrilor. During the first quarter of 1999, the company took a $1,100,000 charge related to a minority investment in a startup company developing ultra-broadband wireless products.

Excluding the acquisition costs and the amortization of intangible assets and goodwill in the first quarter of 2000, and the charge in the first quarter of 1999, pro forma net income for the first quarter of 2000 was $2,708,000, or $.19 per share (diluted), compared to pro forma net income of $1,854,000, or $.15 per share (diluted), for the first quarter of 1999.

Including the acquisition costs and the amortization of intangible assets and goodwill in the first quarter of 2000, and the charge in the first quarter of 1999, net income for the first quarter of 2000 was $2,098,000, or $.14 per share (diluted), compared to net income of $754,000, or $.06 per share (diluted), for the first quarter of 1999. The effective income tax rate, excluding nondeductible charges, is 35% in 2000 and 1999.

"Proxim made progress on a number of important fronts during the first quarter of 2000," said David C. King, Proxim's Chairman, President and CEO. "In home networking, we began initial shipment of HomeRF products to several key partners and entered into a significant joint technology development agreement with Siemens AG to create integrated wideband HomeRF voice and data products. On the corporate development front, Proxim completed the acquisition of Micrilor, a Boston-based developer of direct sequence technology for both the indoor LAN and outdoor point-to-multipoint markets, and began ramping the production process for the Stratum™ ultra-broadband building-to-building products acquired as part of the Wavespan acquisition completed in December 1999. In Q1 2000, we also obtained ISO 9000 certification for our manufacturing operations in California, demonstrating the Company's commitment to producing high quality products for all of our customers worldwide."

About Proxim

Proxim, Inc. has more than 15 years of experience in the wirefree broadband networking market. Today Proxim's Symphony™ is the world's leading wirefree networking product for the home and small office and the company's RangeLAN2™ leads in the enterprise, OEM and wireless service provider markets. For building-to-building wirefree connectivity, Proxim's Stratum™ offers the fastest license-exempt wirefree Ethernet bridge, connecting buildings at distances up to seven miles.

Proxim is a founding member of the Wireless LAN Interoperability Forum (WLI Forum), established to deliver and test interoperable wireless LAN products and services ( www.wlif.com ). Proxim is also a core member of the Home Radio Frequency Working Group (HomeRF), which is committed to providing a broad range of interoperable wirefree consumer devices ( www.homerf.org ). The Company is headquartered in Sunnyvale, California. See www.proxim.com for additional information.
