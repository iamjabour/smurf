Mentor Graphics Reports Record Results. Fourth Quarter Revenues up 17% Year over Year, Earnings up 55%

WILSONVILLE, Ore., January 31, 2001 - Mentor Graphics Corporation (Nasdaq: MENT) reported fourth quarter 2000 earnings per share of $.43, before acquisition related charges, on revenues of $182 million, compared to revenues of $155 million and earnings per share of $.28 for the fourth quarter of 1999. For the full year 2000, revenues grew 15 percent compared to 1999 growth of 4 percent. Earnings for the year reached $.98 per share before acquisition related charges, compared to $.52 per share for 1999.

Product revenue growth reached 17 percent for the quarter and 16 percent for the full year, compared to 6 percent for the fourth quarter and full year 1999. Total gross margin was a record 81.4 percent, driven by increasing software product sales.

"Mentor Graphics had an excellent 2000 and finished with our best fourth quarter ever," said Walden C. Rhines, chairman of the board and CEO of Mentor Graphics. "We were strong in all our product areas over the year, especially in physical verification and system design software. Backlog continued to build during the quarter. Looking ahead, we see a great opportunity for our new AutoActive® RE product to replace second generation printed circuit board (PCB) routers that are running out of steam. PCB is now in the critical path of electronics design, and AutoActive is the only effective approach to handle the increasingly complex printed circuit boards."

During the fourth quarter Mentor acquired PCB tools from Cadix, extended its leadership in cabling systems with product lines acquired from Harness Software, and purchased a rapid prototyping solution from SpeedGate, which uses field programmable gate arrays to prototype ASICs.

"Although the electronics industry may be slowing, the EDA industry tends to be counter-cyclical to the semiconductor industry and its capital equipment suppliers," said Gregory K. Hinckley, president and COO of Mentor Graphics. "As an illustration, in 1996, the last year of a severe downturn for the semiconductor industry, EDA had a growth year. We see near term strength in our business for the first half of the year compared to the guidance provided last quarter. "

Earnings per share, after acquisition related charges, was $.35 for the fourth quarter, 2000, compared to $.08 for the fourth quarter of 1999. For the full year 2000, earnings per share after acquisition related charges was $.81 compared to $.03 for 1999.

The company will issue a separate release today, after the fourth quarter 2000 results conference call, providing the outlook for the first quarter and full year 2001. That outlook will focus on earnings per share before special charges and amortization of goodwill to provide improved comparability with the way others in the industry report earnings. For comparison, these figures for the fourth quarter and full year 2000 were $.44 and $1.01 respectively. The comparable figures for 1999 were $.29 and $.55.

Mentor Graphics Corporation (Nasdaq: MENT) is a world leader in electronic hardware and software design solutions, providing products and consulting services for the world's most successful electronics and semiconductor companies. Established in 1981, the company reported revenues over the last 12 months of nearly $600 million and employs approximately 2,750 people worldwide. Corporate headquarters are located at 8005 S.W. Boeckman Road, Wilsonville, Oregon 97070-7777; Silicon Valley headquarters are located at 1001 Ridder Park Drive, San Jose, California 95131-2314. World Wide Web site: www.mentor.com .
