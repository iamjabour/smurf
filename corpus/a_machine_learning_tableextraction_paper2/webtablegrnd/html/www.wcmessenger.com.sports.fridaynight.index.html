Friday, November 17, 2000
Chico Dragons (8-2) 3
Petrolia Pirates (7-3) 19
Game Highlights: By LINDEE HAND

Chico?s dream of a long post-season drive in the Class A football playoffs came to an unexpected end in Bowie Friday night. The Dragons were hijacked by the Petrolia Pirates 19-3 in a bi-district showdown.

Coach Don Hasley?s Dragons season ends with an 8-3 record. Petrolia will continue their playoff efforts with an 8-3 mark.

The Pirates were led by two touchdown passes from quarterback Brent King to receiver Aaron Gee. The two connected on scoring plays of 8 and 17 yards.

Meanwhile, Petrolia?s Ross Harrison was also in the spotlight. He kicked field goals of 17 and 23 yards and rushed 17 times for 153 yards.

Petrolia never trailed in the game, jumping out to a 7-0 lead in the first quarter and then adding two field goals in the second quarter for a 13-3 halftime edge.

The Pirates put the game away with a fourth-quarter touchdown.

Chico?s only points came in the second quarter after the offense bogged down at the Petrolia 3-yard line. Colby Loper came on to boot a 20-yard field goal with 6:02 to go, cutting Petrolia?s lead to 7-3.

But that would be as close as the Dragons could get against the Pirates.

It marked the second win of the year for Petrolia over Chico. The Pirates knocked off the Dragons 34-21 in the third week of the regular season.

Game Highlights: By Mitch Word

If football was compared to a three-legged sack race, the Bridgeport Bulls came up short Friday after losing two of its most powerful legs.

The Bulls fell to Sweetwater 38-27 in a Class 3A Division II playoff contest at Tarleton State University Stadium in Stephenville.

The loss to the Mustangs ended Bridgeport?s season at 6-5, but only after a gutsy performance from an offense hampered by the loss of running back Jesse Stowers. Stowers went out midway through the second period with a leg injury and never returned.

However, Bridgeport quarterback Phillip Daugherty stepped up with a 253-yard passing performance.

?Offensively, we played a great game. We had a lot of people stepping up and filling roles,? said Bulls Head Coach Danny Henson after the game.

Eventually, it was the Bulls? defensive effort that failed to stop a Sweetwater running attack that amassed 275 yards.

?We tackled poorly,? Henson said. ?Defensively we played too horribly to be out there. We had missed tackles, missed assignments, and gave up a lot of long drives.?

Northwest Texans (6-4) 14
Wichita Falls Coyotes (10-0) 28
Game Highlights: By RANDY SACHS

The Wichita Falls Coyotes, the state?s top-ranked Class 4A football team, ran into a Northwest High School Texan team that battled from the opening kickoff to the final gun in a bi-district showdown in Wichita Falls Friday night.

The undefeated Coyotes moved up the playoff ladder with a hard-fought 28-14 win over the Texans. Wichita Falls is now 11-0.

Northwest ends a successful season with a 6-5 mark. The playoff was the first for the Texans since 1989.

?We never let up,? said Northwest Coach Terry Goode. ?If we had gotten a couple of breaks, well, you never know how it might have turned out.

?Wichita Falls is certainly going to give a lot of people trouble ? they already gave our 11 guys plenty of it tonight.

?Still, I was proud of our team. The defense hung in there and played tough. This is going to help build our program.?
Boyd Yellowjackets (4-6) 0
Celina Bobcats (10-0) 38
Game Highlights: By Johnnie and Roberta Campbell

The Celina Bobcats lived up to their reputation as a perennial high school football powerhouse. The two-time defending state Class 2A Division II champions crushed the Boyd Yellowjackets 38-0 in a bi-district playoff game Friday at Denton High School Stadium.

?They got on us real quick. I don?t know if we were ready,? said Yellowjacket Coach J. G. Cartwright. ?We?ve had to play real hard to overcome adversity all season long.?

On the first play of the game, Celina?s Josh Sharrock ran 74 yards for a touchdown and Grant May added the extra point. Less than three minutes later, Adam Harvey scored, this time on a 6-yard run to give Celina a 14-0 lead.

Boyd came back, driving to the Celina 5-yard line with a 75-yard march behind quarterback Brian Harmon?s scrambling. But, the drive stalled and a field goal was wide right to end the scoring threat.

Things went from bad to worse for the Yellowjackets as halfway through the first quarter they lost Harmon to an injury.

The Bobcats scored again in the second quarter on a 21-yard run by Harvey and at half-time Celina led 21-0.
