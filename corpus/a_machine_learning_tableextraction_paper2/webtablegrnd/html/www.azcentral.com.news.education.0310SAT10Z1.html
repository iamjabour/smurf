Student's SAT score perfect

1 of only 5 in state to score 1,600

Kristin Koch

The Arizona Republic

Mar. 10, 2001

Mary Lou Retton and former New Yankees pitcher Don Larsen know what it's like to be perfect.

Matt du Mee must know how they feel.

The 18-year-old Glendale student recently scored a perfect 1,600 on his SATs. That puts du Mee in select company. In the 1999-2000 school year, 19,914 people in Arizona took the SATs, and only five scored 1,600.

"It was a total surprise for me," du Mee said. "I was in a daze the whole day. I couldn't concentrate on anything."

Du Mee attends Northwest Community Church, 16615 N. 43rd Ave., Phoenix, and is one of 16 students enrolled in the faith-based Northwest Family High School, which is held on the church grounds.

Du Mee attributes his perfect score to working "as hard as I can in whatever situation I am in, because I feel that glorifies God. I try to be serious with it (school), but I also try to have fun."

He also has been perfect in his grades - well, almost perfect. He has always been a straight-A student, except for one B he got in physical education.

Du Mee attends school Monday through Thursday for four hours each day and says he gets homework in every class every day. The homework, he says, is what keeps him busy.

"It is really a good challenge," he says.

He admits that he is a very competitive person but says he works as hard as he does in an effort to glorify God.

Also keeping him busy are the two math classes he teaches twice a week at his school. This semester, he is teaching Logic/Problem Solving and Algebra I. Last semester, he taught Pre-Calculus and Algebra I.

"It's kind of weird," he says of teaching his peers. "I've never really taught before. It's weird to be in a position of authority over them. Then class finishes and I am just a friend and fellow peer again."

Du Mee hopes to attend Patrick Henry College in Virginia next fall to pursue a career in politics.

"I'd really like to make a difference in that matter," he said of politics. He hopes someday to be president of the United States.
