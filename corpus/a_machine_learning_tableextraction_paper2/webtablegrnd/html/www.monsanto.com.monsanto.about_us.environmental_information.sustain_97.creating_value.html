People Define It Differently




Creating Value Through Sustainable Development

Many people are beginning to appreciate more deeply the bonds between human well-being, social stability and the natural processes of earth that sustain all life. They are realizing that the earth’s capacity to continue providing clean air and water, productive soils, and a rich diversity of plant and animal life is central to ensuring quality of life for ourselves and our descendants.

This awareness is captured in the concept of "sustainable development". It is a concept still evolving. People define it differently. Most include ideas of economic growth, of improved quality of life, and of conserving resources and protecting the environment for future generations.

Today, both for us and others, the issue of how to help make sustainable development happen globally remains something of a puzzle. We know some pieces well. But other pieces, and the connections among them, still are vague.

The conditions of our world – the stresses being placed on natural and social systems that sustain us – eventually will require all of us to solve the sustainable development puzzle. Monsanto is determined to be a leader among businesses in this effort. We committed to this course several years ago, and we think our scientific and technological skills can provide us special opportunities to have an impact.

We believe pursuing sustainable development is about more than merely surviving, or simply reducing the harmful effects of what we do. The more we consider it, the more we conclude that there is great opportunity to create value for our customers, suppliers, shareholders, ourselves and the world at large.

This value exists on a continuum, ranging from near-term to long-term, from our bottom line to the world’s poverty line. There is financial value, both in terms of money we save by making our operations more eco-efficient and in new market opportunities created by a world pursuing sustainable development. Increasingly, our products are creating value for our customers by helping them combine profitability with environmental stewardship. There is also value for the developing world, where first steps are being taken to strengthen local economies. From these efforts new markets may grow. But there is no hope for new markets until people can first meet their most basic needs.

This report describes how Monsanto people are working to create value from sustainable development and how much more we have to learn about it.
