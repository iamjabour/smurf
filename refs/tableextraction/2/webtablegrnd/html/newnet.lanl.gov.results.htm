Interpreting your results

Because of the design of the calculator, your estimated annual radiation dose is going to be between 299 mrem and 8712 mrem. The majority of the high-end doses are for those people who have identified themselves as cigarette smokers. A non-smoker will find their annual dose to be between 299 and 712 mrem, which can be compared to the national average dose of 360 mrem per year.

For comparison, the following are the annual dose limits as specified by the government for different people. These are limits, and do not include environmental or background radiation, which you just calculated. That is, a radiation worker can receive both their background dose and an occupational dose up to the legal dose limit. Additional comparisons are available in the previous section, What is a "mrem?" Note that these values are for the highest dose limits a person can legally be exposed to in a year. For a more realistic value, the radiation workers at the Department of Energy receive about 44 mrem per year, even though they could legally receive 5, 000 mrem per year.

Person Dose Limit
Radiation Worker 5, 000 mrem
Pregnant Radiation Worker 500 mrem
Member of the Public 100 mrem

These numbers may seem high when compared to background levels. However, a person must receive 25, 000 mrem before any physical effect can be seen. At a dose of 25, 000 mrem, the only noticeable effect is seen in a laboratory analysis of the person's bloodcount. The person would have to receive a dose of around 200, 000 mrem before they would notice any effect themselves.
