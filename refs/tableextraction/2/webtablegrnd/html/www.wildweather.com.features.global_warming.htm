Congratulations. Thanks to the effort of millions of people just like you scientists tell us we have started the inevitable process of altering the climate of planet Earth.

The evidence is hard to ignore. Climatologists report that the Earth has warmed by about a 1.0 F (0.6 Celsius) since 1900. If we keep up our bad habits the average global temperature could increase as much as 6.0 F (3.5 Celsius during the next century). Not long ago wintertime actually meant cold and snow for some people. These days many Eskimos are taking surfing lessons.



The Problem:

In a nutshell the problem is human activity. Every time we start an engine, chop down a tree, or start our own herd of cattle greenhouse gases are emitted. These gases, which include carbon dioxide, methane, ozone, halocarbons and nitrous oxide, let in sunlight but tend to insulate the planet against the loss of heat, not unlike the glass of a greenhouse.

While all the greenhouse gases present problems, the biggest villain is carbon dioxide. Carbon dioxide has a nasty habit of remaining trapped in our atmosphere, sometimes for centuries. Over time these gases start to build and slowly warm our atmosphere. During the 1980's human beings released 7.2 billion tons of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere each year. Since then things have gone up, up and away.

The Future:

Nobody knows for sure what will happen this century but plenty of well-trained scientists suggest that things could really start to get interesting in the next few decades. While many of the changes would be negative a few could be beneficial. Here are some possible scenarios, both good and bad:



BAD - A warming planet could radically alter natural patterns of vegetation and animal habitats that have taken thousands of years to become established.

GOOD - A warming planet might expand the length and geographical reach of human cultivation. Perhaps fresh vine ripened tomatoes from the Russian tundra will no longer be out of the question.

BAD - Rising sea levels could place millions of people at risk as their countries join the lost city of Atlantis.

GOOD - One perk; the weather should become even wilder as an atmosphere clearly out of equilibrium struggles to even things out through massive temperature changes, powerful hurricanes and possibly more twisters.

BAD / GOOD - Melting ice fields will permit archeologists to dig up all kinds of fascinating artifacts from areas that were previously off limits. Maybe we will be able to firmly establish whether Leprechauns once roamed the planet.

BAD / GOOD - Insurance companies everywhere will take a second look before insuring that home with the million dollar view of the ocean.
