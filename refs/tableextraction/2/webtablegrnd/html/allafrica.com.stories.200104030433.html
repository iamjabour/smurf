African Soccer Magazine (London)

April 3, 2001

> Posted to the web April 3, 2001

Eric Odanga

> Nairobi

Continental engagements played havoc with the fixture list of Kenya's Premier League at the weekend, with Mathare United and Oserian Fastac respectively in action in the Cup Winners' Cup and CAF Cup competitions. The result is to give the table an even more lopsided look than it had before, with some sides having played seven games and others only two.

This time, the ripples spread to affect Ulinzi Stars, Kenya Commercial Bank, Tusker FC and Nakuru Police, who were supposed to play at the Afraha Stadium, the belated venue of the CAF Cup match between Oserian Fastac and Arabica de Kirundo. That game had been scheduled for Oserian's home ground in Naivasha, but Ugandan match commissioner Christopher Rwanika said at the 11th hour that the venue was unfit for a continental fixture.

Mathare could see their maiden African adventure draw to a premature close, after being held 1-1 by Egypt's Ismailia at the Moi International Sports Centre, Kasarani. Oserian had better luck, though, coming from behind to beat their Burundian opponents 2-1. Return legs of both ties will be played on the weekend of April 13-15.

Defending champions Tusker FC should have been in action in Africa, too, but their first-round CAF Champions' League tie against St George FC of Ethiopia was postponed for a week to make way for the final of the African Youth Championship in Addis Ababa.

Instead, therefore, Tusker turned their attention back home, winning in their first victory in only two league appearances, 3-1 over lowly Red Berets in Limuru. They were barely stretched, even after losing two key players, John Muiruri and Robert Mambo, to Belgian first division side Gent.

Mumias Sugar are still unbeaten after a 1-0 win against Sony Sugar in Awendo. It was Mark Sirengo who scored the decisive goal, pushing his goal tally to three in as many matches.

After a promising start, Kisima Bombers appear to have forgotten how to score goals as they slumped to a 1-3 defeat at the hands of Utalii. Despite protests by Kalayia Muna, the Kisima Bombers team manager, the win was enough to move Utalii five places up in the league table.

Even at this early stage of the season, the first managerial head has rolled. Nzoia Sugar fired their coach, Noah Wanyama, after notching up only two draws and two defeats in four games. His place has been taken by player-coach Evans Kadenge. Nzoia face Nakuru Police next weekend in Nakuru.

League leaders AFC Leopards have meanwhile named former international defender Mickey Weche as their coach.

The biggest news, though, for Kenyan sports fans is the arrival of the legendary English footballer, Sir Bobby Charlton, and hurdler Edwin Moses on Friday, to coincide with the opening of the new Mathare Youth Community Centre. Mathare, a club born out of a widely-praised slum development project, will be hoping some of the VIPs' charisma wears off when they travel to Egypt for their tricky Cup Winners' Cup return match against Ismailia.
