Understanding Search Results


When you search with Lycos, our software compares the words you enter in the Search Box with the words found on millions of pages across the Web. Lycos gathers a list of Web pages that matches your search terms, adds everything we know about resources related to that topic, and organizes all that information on the Search Results page. The following information will help you understand how to navigate around a Lycos Search Results page and get the information you want:

1. Advanced Search
Popular topics and common words can generate pages and pages of results when searching with Lycos. If your first search attempt digs up an overwhelming number of suggested links, you can refine your results with Lycos Advanced Search. These flexible tools allow you to fine-tune your search with exact phrase logic and other fancy options. You can also focus on specific content areas, like audio files, FTP downloads, newsgroup postings, digital pictures, and recipes. You can learn more about these advanced options by clicking here.



2. Suggested Searches



The Suggested Searches feature was created to help people whose search terms were too general or too specific to return the most relevant results. Other sites use "related searches" that are simply derivatives of your original search term(s); we take the process further by matching your query with data created by your fellow users. For example, users who searched for "Beatles" also searched for "Metallica," "Freeware," and "John Lennon." These associated queries can help point you to the right information; plus, it's interesting to see what else people who searched on your query are looking for. The results are generated by gathering a consensus of data from the search habits of the Web community, not from an individual's search refinement patterns.



3. Popular Links region
For our most popular search terms and topics, we have put together a group of links designed to give you the content you want in a quick and efficient manner. You'll see this at the top of the search results page under the heading Popular , and we think you'll find it to be a great resource for speedy answers. This section will not come up for every search, but the Lycos search team will review our search records on a daily basis and gather the most popular links to meet the needs of our customers.

4. Search Results sections
When you search with Lycos, the results will often span multiple pages and cover all types of Web media: news items, hobby pages, dedicated Web sites, and so on. The Lycos Search Results page has been designed to give you the most information in an easy-to-understand layout, without making you wait several minutes for the page to load. It's definitely fast and easy! To help us categorize the results, our software organizes the Web pages by type. After an incredible amount of mixing and matching, the results are sorted into three main sections: Web Sites, News Articles, and Shopping. You can navigate through these sections by using the links in each section's heading. The name of the section you are currently browsing will be highlighted, and you can easily access the other sections by clicking their names.





5. Search Guard
Many Lycos customers appreciate the protection offered by SearchGuard, a free Lycos service that lets customers filter adult material from their searches. SearchGuard technology was developed by Lycos to allow a safe searching environment for anyone who does not want to be exposed to adult content and banner ads while searching with Lycos. This service combines the ability to restrict search terms and limit the relevance rating of Web sites that are identified as containing adult content. You can also forbid access to Chat, Email, and Message Boards on Lycos with Search Guard. You can learn more by following this link.



6. Web Sites region
This section is populated with links from several different sources: Web pages that were manually submitted to Lycos, world-class Web sites chosen by volunteer editors for the Lycos Open Directory, and general categories in the Open Directory that match your search phrase.







7. News Articles region
If you want to research a breaking news story, the Web Sites section may not have what you're looking for. Scroll down to this section to get up-to-the-minute information from the Web's leading news sites.





8. "More" links
Navigation links are located at bottom of each section to help you move through multiple pages of results. Click on More Web Sites or More News Articles to view additional resources in those particular areas:

Once you have done that, it's easy to navigate through the additional links in our search results. Check the bottom of each page for self-explanatory Back and Next links that allow you to browse through the results one page at a time.

9. Shopping region
Do you have cash burning a hole in your pocket? There's no need to browse through dozens of links to find an online store willing to take your money! Take a look at the Shopping section to find appropriate links in LYCOShop, the online shopping center for Web users everywhere.
