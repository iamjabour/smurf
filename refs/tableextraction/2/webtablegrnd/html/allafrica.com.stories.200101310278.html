Deployment Of ECOWAS Troops Behind Schedule



>
> > > >
>

Panafrican News Agency

January 31, 2001

> Posted to the web January 31, 2001

Segun Adeyemi

> Lagos, Nigeria

The deployment of ECOWAS force advance team to the borders of Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone has fallen behind schedule, threatening the mandate of the peacekeeping mission.

Senior military officials from the four troop contributing countries had agreed at a meeting in Abuja to deploy the advance team by 27 January, while the entire force of more than 1,700 was expected to be operational within a month of the initial deployment.

ECOWAS sources told PANA Wednesday the delay in the appointment of a force commander for the mission might have stalled the deployment.

The force commander is expected to come from Nigeria, the largest troop contributor to the peacekeeping mission with 776 soldiers.

However, Nigeria's Defence Spokesman, Brig-Gen. Godwin Ugbo, told PANA the force commander had been appointed and was awaiting confirmation by ECOWAS Heads of State and Government.

"Hopefully, he will be confirmed before Friday," said Ugbo, who insists the identity of the force commander would not be made public until the confirmation.

Meanwhile, the ECOWAS Executive Secretary, Lansana Kouyate, has stepped up efforts to put the troops on the ground as soon as possible.

Kouyate is currently on a trip to Guinea to consult with President Lansana Conte, the Guinean government and the Army Chief of Staff on how to implement the ECOWAS decision to deploy the mission.

The ECOWAS Defence and Security Commission approved the deployment 28 December 2000 in Abuja, following escalating activities of insurgents operating along the common borders of the three countries.

The force is mandated to restore security and confidence in the area, and to use force if necessary to deter aggression or violation of the borders in the exercise of its mandate.

In addition to Nigeria, other troop-contributing countries are Mali (500 troops), Senegal (220) and Niger (200).
