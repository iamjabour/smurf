VERITY REPORTS RECORD THIRD QUARTER RESULTS; MAINTAINS ITS PROJECTIONS FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FISCAL YEAR



Financial Highlights

  • Revenue up 10% over prior quarter, to a record $38.0 million

  • Net income on a fully taxed basis up 13% over prior quarter, to a record $9.2 million

  • Record EPS of $0.25 on a fully taxed, fully diluted basis

  • 12th consecutive profitable quarter

SUNNYVALE, CA. ? March 14, 2001 - Verity, Inc. (NASDAQ: VRTY), the company powering business portals, today reported financial results for its fiscal quarter ended February 28, 2001. Verity?s revenues for its third fiscal quarter were a record $38.0 million. Software product revenues, which represented 76% of total revenues for the quarter, were a record $28.7 million. Net income for the quarter on a fully taxed basis was a record $9.2 million, or $0.25 per diluted share.



"This performance serves as a continuing testimony that our strategy is working, and provides further proof of the growing acceptance of Verity by companies seeking to utilize best of breed components to build their business portal infrastructure," said Gary J. Sbona, Verity's chairman and chief executive officer. "Our experience, high quality products and services and a growing roster of satisfied global customers have helped us achieve leadership in the growing business portal market and maintain an edge across all our product segments."



Verity also announced that it has extended its management agreement with Regent Pacific Management Corporation through August 31, 2002. Under the amended agreement, Regent Pacific continues to provide the services of Gary J. Sbona as chairman and chief executive officer of Verity, and continues to provide additional Regent Pacific management services to the Company. The agreement provides Verity with an option to further extend the term of this agreement through February 2003.



Mr. Sbona has been Verity's chief executive officer since July 1997 and has been a board member since May 1998. He has been chairman of its board of directors since March 1999.



"Over the past three and a half years, Gary Sbona has made Verity a significant force in the industry by focusing its attention on sustained profitable growth," said Steven M. Krausz, a member of Verity?s board of directors and a partner with U.S. Venture Partners. "By extending our agreement with Regent Pacific we endorse the tremendous progress that Verity has made under the leadership and direction of Gary and his team."



During the third quarter, Verity realized significant sales from all its core markets, including Intranet portal and e-commerce solutions for major retail, telecommunications, banking and financial services, publishing, professional services, pharmaceutical, aerospace, airlines and high technology companies. Verity attributes this success, in part, to its continued competitive advantages: exceptional experience in providing the infrastructure powering business portals, intelligent solutions, improved efficiency and communications for its customers as well as their customers, and top-notch technology.



On a nine-month year-to-date basis, Verity's revenues were $103.8 million, up 58% from the same period last year. Beginning this fiscal year, Verity's financial results have been reported on a fully taxed basis. Net income for the first nine months was $24.6 million, or $0.67 per share. For the same nine-month period last year, Verity?s pro-forma net income was $12.1 million, or $0.35 per share, on a comparable fully taxed basis. Net income on a comparable fully taxed basis increased 103% over the same period last year.



Business Outlook for remainder of Fiscal 2001

Verity also released the following projections concerning its future operations. Readers are cautioned that these projections are forward-looking statements subject to a number of risks and uncertainties as explained more fully below, and actual results could be materially different.



For the remaining quarter of FY 2001, Verity projects that it will continue to achieve financial results consistent with its annual operating plan.



Verity projects its fourth quarter revenues to increase 8% to 12% on a sequential quarterly basis, which translates to projected Q4 revenues between $41.0 and $42.5 million.



Verity projects fourth quarter earnings per share, on a fully taxed/fully diluted basis, to be between $0.24 and $0.27 per share.



In total, Verity projects revenues for FY 2001 to be between $144.8 million and $146.3 million, which equates to a 51% to 52% increase from FY 2000 revenues of $96.1 million.



Verity projects net income for FY 2001 to be between $33.7 million and $34.9 million on a fully taxed basis. This equates to a 73% to 79% increase from FY 2000 net income of $19.5 million on a comparable fully taxed basis.

Verity projects total year earnings per share for FY 2001 to be between $0.91 and $0.94 per share on a fully taxed/fully diluted basis, compared to $0.56 per share on a comparable fully taxed/fully diluted basis for FY 2000.

About Verity

Verity is a leader in powering business portals. Verity gives businesses a multitude of ways to improve access to vital information, perform a range of e-business operations, while enhancing the end-user experience. Business portals, which include corporate portals used for sharing information within an enterprise, e-commerce portals for online selling and market exchange portals for B2B activities, provide personalized information to employees, partners, customers and suppliers.



Verity products are used by 53 of the Fortune 100 and by more than 1,000 corporations worldwide. The markets Verity serves are comprised of e-businesses, e-commerce merchants, market exchanges, government agencies, online service providers, Internet publishers and software developers. Clients include Adobe Systems, American

Greetings, AT&T, BroadVision, CDNOW, Chapters, Cisco, CNET, Compaq, Dow Jones, Edgar Online, Ernst & Young, Fairmarket, Financial Times, Globe and Mail, Home Depot, IBM, Lotus, NewsEdge Corporation, SAP, Siemens, Snap.com, Sybase, Time New Media and Timex.
