NHL: Bure fails in bid to score 60 goals; Panthers beat Rangers 3-0

Sunday, April 8, 2001

Associated Press

SUNRISE — Kevyn Adams, Denis Shvidki and Paul Laus scored in a 1:26 span of the third period as the Florida Panthers beat the New York Rangers 3-0 on Saturday in the season finale for both teams.

Florida's Pavel Bure, who leads the NHL with 59 goals, failed in his attempt to score 60 for the third time in his career. He was held without a point.

Roberto Luongo stopped all 25 New York shots for his franchise-record fifth shutout of the season.

Neither team was able to score through the first 50:01, but the Panthers quickly took over the game with three quick goals.

Adams notched his 11th of the year when he rebounded a miss by Shvidki, who's shot from the slot glanced off the left post and bounded out, over Guy Hebert at 9:59.

Just 17 seconds later the Panthers made it 2-0 when Shvidki connected past Hebert.

Laus followed up with his first goal of the year when Marcus Nilson sent a pass trickling through the crease. Laus took the puck at the bottom of the right circle and sent it past Hebert.

The Panthers had the best chance to score in the first period. Bure got the puck at the top of the right circle and skated in alone on Hebert who was able to snag the puck midway through the first.

The Rangers had a pair of quality chances in the second period, but were denied both times.

At 11:47 Valeri Kamensky got out on a three-on-two breakaway and fed Michal Grosek who was trailing behind. But Luongo blocked Grosek's point-blank shot from the low slot. At 16:05 Grosek would just miss connecting when the puck went over the crossbar.

Two seconds later Florida's Lance Pitlick checked New York's Mike Mottau into the boards setting off a major scrum. But the Rangers only had two minutes of a one-man advantage and failed to come close to scoring.

Bure broke the NHL record for percentage of a team's goals. Bure accounted for 29.5 percent of Florida's scores (59 of 200). Brett Hull set the mark when he scored 27.7 percent St. Louis' goals (86 of 310) in 1990-91. ... Even with Bure, the Panthers finished with the fewest goals in their history, except for the lockout season, with 200. That eclipsed the mark of 203 in 1997-98. ... With 22 wins, the Panthers finished with the fewest victories in a full season in team history. The previous low was 24 in the 1997-98 season. ... Rangers right wing Radek Dvorak has skated in 269 consecutive games, third-best among active players. ... The Rangers have missed the playoffs four straight years.
