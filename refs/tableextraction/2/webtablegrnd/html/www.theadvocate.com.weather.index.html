Southeastern Louisiana is in a drought again, with more of the state headed that direction, State Climatologist Jay Grymes said. A near-record drought gripped south Louisiana last summer.

Federal environmental officials have officially outlined what's at stake for the five-parish Baton Rouge area if its ozone problem is reclassified from "serious" to "severe."

The Louisiana Environmental Action Network, an environmental activist group, is asking a federal judge here to direct the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency to classify the five-parish Baton Rouge area as a "severe" ozone violator.



Officials say lower Mississippi River probably won't flood (4/24/01)

Communities along the lower Mississippi River are likely to escape the major flooding that's now hitting the Midwest, state emergency officials say.

With high winds whipping the Mississippi River into waves that battered levees and buildings, volunteers and National Guard soldiers battled Monday to keep the river from overwhelming flood defenses.

Midwest battles threat of flooding rivers (4/16/01)

The swollen Red River dropped slowly Sunday along the Minnesota-North Dakota state line, where cities frantically have reinforced dikes against flooding. However, the river is expected to stay high and above flood stage for several days.

Damaging wind gusts eased across the upper Midwest on Sunday in the aftermath of a storm that ripped down power lines and overturned tractor-trailer rigs, but flood preparations were under way as rivers started rising out of their banks.
