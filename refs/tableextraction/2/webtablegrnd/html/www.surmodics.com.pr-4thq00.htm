Record Profits on 35% Annual Revenue Growth

EDEN PRAIRIE, Minnesota ? October 26, 2000 - SurModics, Inc. (Nasdaq: SRDX), a leading provider of surface modification solutions to medical device manufacturers, today reported its financial results for the fourth quarter and fiscal year ended September 30, 2000.

Fiscal year 2000 revenue increased 35% to $18.3 million from $13.5 million in fiscal 1999. Double-digit growth occurred across all PhotoLink â revenue categories, as each established new annual records. In total, PhotoLink revenue increased 60% for the year, led by a 73% increase in PhotoLink royalties. Income from operations increased 120% to $5.3 million, and net income was $4.2 million, or $.50 per diluted share, an 85% increase over the $2.3 million, or $.28 per diluted share, reported last year (excluding the reversal of an income tax valuation reserve totaling $2.1 million in 1999).

"Fiscal 2000 was a very productive year for SurModics," said Dale Olseth, Chairman and CEO. "We delivered on last year?s objectives of growing profits and expanding our technology base beyond slippery coatings. We produced record revenues and profits, and once again, PhotoLink was key to the Company?s performance. This growth was positively impacted by the initial $1.0 million license fee from Motorola BioChip Systems, but all other PhotoLink revenue sources also showed large increases, led by the 73% growth in royalties. These increases demonstrate that our licensing-based business model works, and it should continue to generate increasing earnings in the future."

"Fiscal 2000 was also a success from a technical perspective," said Jim Powell, President and Chief Operating Officer. "The most visible indicator of that success was the genomics license and development agreement signed with Motorola. We are excited about the prospects for this partnership. SurModics? people are working closely with Motorola personnel to get their first generation genomics product on the market. In addition, we are making significant progress with the Cordis Division of Johnson & Johnson on a stent coating for the controlled release of drugs to combat restenosis. This coronary stent has entered human clinical trials in Europe, and early results look very promising. Both of these projects represent significant opportunities for us in the future."

Other fiscal 2000 highlights include the following:

  • Operating margins have shown steady improvement. Margins increased to 29% in fiscal 2000 from 18% last year, and 10% in fiscal 1998.
  • Three new license agreements were signed during the fourth quarter, bringing the total to 10 for the fiscal year. SurModics now has license agreements with 49 companies covering more than 100 product applications.
  • Clients currently have 47 PhotoLink-coated products on the market generating royalty revenue for SurModics. In addition, SurModics has performed coating work for human clinical studies on an additional nine products during the past quarter. Four of these applications are using lubricity, but SurModics? newer coating features are gaining acceptance (three hemocompatibility applications, one antimicrobial and one drug incorporation). None of these products have yet reached the market.
  • SurModics ended the year with a strong balance sheet including approximately $40 million of cash and investments, and no debt.

Revenue for the fourth quarter increased 43% to $5.5 million from $3.9 million in the comparable period of fiscal 1999. The primary factor in this increase was the $1.0 million license fee received from Motorola. Net income was $1.5 million, or $.18 per diluted share, a 126% increase over the $677,000, or $.08 per diluted share, reported last year (excluding the reversal of an income tax valuation reserve totaling $440,000).

SurModics, Inc., a leading provider of surface modification solutions, licenses its patented photoactivated coating process to medical device manufacturers around the world. A signification portion of SurModics? revenue is generated through royalties on the sale of coated products. SurModics? Internet address is www.surmodics.com .
