User Training

Our beginner's courses provide the road-map to GOCAD: friendly and comprehensive lectures and exercises that allow a new user to go through the basic tools and concepts in GOCAD with ease. The goal of these three courses (GOCAD Quick Start, Basics in GOCAD 1, and Basics in GOCAD 2) is to familiarize the user with the GOCAD Style and to establish the user's confidence in mastering such an advanced application.

Advanced courses are subject-oriented, focusing on developing knowledge and skills in a particular field of model building. We offer four courses, with emphases on structural modeling, reservoir property modeling, velocity modeling, and most importantly, how to build a common earth model that is shared by all disciplines of exploration and production.

If you are a seasoned GOCAD user already, you may want to consider the unique one-day course in Wizard-programming.

In addition, we offer special one-day workshops where we use your project to show you modeling techniques that are of immediate relevance to your project.

  • Gocad Quick Start (GQS) : Introduction to the GOCAD user interface and the GOCAD style; common modeling objects such as horizons, faults, and wells; visualization.

  • Basics in GOCAD 1 (BIG1) : How to use Important features and applications for geologists, geophysicists, or reservoir engineers; more on common modeling objects.

  • Geostatistics And Reservoir Modeling In Gocad, part I (GARCIG 1) : Overview of the geostatistical tools in GOCAD, such as variogram analysis and Sequential Gaussian Simulation.

  • Geostatistics And Reservoir Modeling In Gocad, part II (GARCIG 2) : A half-day course in nested variogram modeling, geostatistical velocity modeling, and more.

  • Horizon and Fault Modeling in Gocad (HAFMIG 1) : Overview of important issues in structural modeling, such as data manipulation, modeling options, Wizards, and more.

  • Faulted Integrated Reservoir Modeling (FIRM) : A half-day course in handling faults when modeling a reservoir and reservoir properties, including constructing faulted SGrid (stratigraphic grids).

  • Special Workshops : Subjects and contents are determined by your projects.

Developer Training

GOCAD is designed to support proprietary development. We offer three different levels of training to get you started in the exciting arena of GOCAD-programming. We do recommend that you complete a 3-day beginning users training since familiarity with the GOCAD user interface is highly advantageous.
  • Wizard Programming: This unique one-day training (a wizard is workflow-oriented macro) is designed for anyone who can use GOCAD; programming knowledge is not required! We highly recommend this course to both users and developers.

  • Introductory Course: This two-day class offers C++ programmers a broad overview of the GOCAD library and thorough explanations of the key concepts in various technologies.

  • Intermediate Course: This two-day course provides more in-depth practices in utilizing existing library and creating new classes and functions.

For details, see the Table of Contents of the Developer's Training Manual.

Courses features

Organized Training Material

All our regular training courses are based on structured training manuals which consist of a series of informative lectures and stimulating hands-on exercises, complete with summary and discussion sections to encourage questions.

Small class sizes

In all our regular training sessions, student number is limited to 8 (eight) to ensure individual attention. Students are paired up (two students per computer) to encourage discussion and team work.

Experienced instructors

Our training classes are lead by GOCAD instructors who are experts on the subjects. They are familiar with both GOCAD and the issues that a beginning user or programmer in GOCAD may face.

Special Workshops

In addition to our regular training courses, we offer Special Workshops that are tailored to meet specific needs. Contents of a Special Workshop can be based on a particular project or certain subjects that are of special interest to you.

On-site training

All our regular training courses and special workshops can be conducted on-site.

How to enroll

To reserve a seat in a class, please contact us via email ( training@gocad.com ), telephone (713-787-0746 x-11, 33 03 83 67 66 44), or fax (713-787-0852, 33 03 83 67 66 34). You will then complete the registration form and send it to T-Surf via fax, or postal service. Full payment or a payment agreement must be received by T-Surf no later than two weeks prior to the starting date of the intended training session or the reservation is no longer guaranteed.

Cancellation

A cancellation is fully refundable (less US$50 processing fee) if the notice of cancellation is received by T-Surf at least 2 weeks prior to the starting date of the intended class.

No refund will be issued after this 2-week cancellation deadline.

Transfer

A request to transfer to a later class (provided that there is an opening) must be made no later than 5 days prior to the starting date of the currently reserved class.

Any request made later than this date is considered a late cancellation and will not be refunded.

T-Surf Cancellation

Under rare circumstances, such as but not limited to, severe weather or insufficient enrollment, T-Surf reserves the right to cancel a course.

If a cancellation is necessary, it will be announced as early as possible. A fully registered participant can either receive a full refund or transfer to a later class that still has an opening.
