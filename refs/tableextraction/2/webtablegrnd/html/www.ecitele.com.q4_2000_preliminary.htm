ECI Telecom Expects Shortfall In Fourth Quarter 2000 Results

De-Merger Faster Than Expected

PETAH TIKVA, ISRAEL, December 14, 2000, ECI Telecom Ltd. (NASDAQ/NM symbol: ECIL) announced today that based on a preliminary analysis of fourth quarter results, the Company expects to report Q4 2000 revenues in the range of US$ 310 - 330 million and not as previously expected.

The shortfall in revenue is attributable to a greater than expected decline in legacy DCME products and less than expected growth of Optical Network products.

The Company's earnings will be negatively impacted by the shortfall in revenues and a larger loss than previously expected in Access Solutions products. As a result, the company expects to report an operating loss of $15 -25 million before restructuring and one-time charges.

"Obviously we are disappointed with the expected operational results," said Doron Inbar , ECI Telecom's CEO and President. "However, our strategic plan is on track and has accelerated. The de-merger process is proceeding faster than our earlier expectation. Furthermore, it is our highest priority to pave the way for our 5 new companies to begin separate reporting as of Q1 2001. During the fourth quarter, we faced the challenge of both delivering operating results and at the same time, carving out the new companies. These companies will begin their separate and independent operations as of January 1, 2001. Primarily in connection with the execution of the de-merger plan, the Company expects to include restructuring and one-time costs in its Q4 results".

"While we are preparing to take these companies public, we are also in active discussions with strategic financial and technology investors, who are attracted by our de-merger program, and have the potential of creating value for our shareholders".

About ECI Telecom

ECI Telecom Ltd. is a provider of integrated network solutions for digital communications and data transmission systems. The Company designs, develops, manufactures, markets and supports end-to-end digital telecommunications solutions for today's new services and converging networks. The Company's products create bandwidth, maximize revenues for network operators, expand capacity, improve performance and enable new revenue-producing services. In doing so, they enhance the capabilities of existing networks to support voice, data, video and multimedia services. ECI Telecom's equipment supports traffic in more than 500 service networks in over 145 countries.
