Voters to decide on council, school bonds

Polls open Monday in Troy, Bloomfield; Tuesday in Berkley



School bond election

The Berkley School District will ask voters Tuesday to approve the following bond proposals.

* A $10.3-million bond to refurbish Berkley High School and add technology improvements to all other district schools.

* A $5.4-million bond for athletic and physical education facility improvements.
By Jennifer Brooks / The Detroit News



TROY -- Voters here will go to the polls Monday to elect a mayor and two city council members.

The polls in the nonpartisan election also will be open in Bloomfield Hills, where three candidates are running unopposed for the city commission, and in Pleasant Ridge, where incumbent Mayor Ralph A. Castelli Jr. is running unopposed and four candidates are vying for two positions on the city commission.

Troy voters will chose a new mayor from three candidates: James J. Walker, 62, a registered stock broker and retirement counselor for Edward Jones Investments; Henry Allemon, 58, a vice-president for Midwest Guaranty Bank; and Matt Pryor, 40, the owner of Quality Landscaping Design & Construction.

On Tuesday, Berkley voters will decide two school bond issues. There will be no tax increase if a $10.3 million bond is approved because school officials will maintain the current bond retirement millage rate. The rate will be reduced if the bond is not approved.

Approval of a $5.4 million bond proposal would increase taxes by 0.5 mill. If approved, the owner of a home valued at $200,000 would pay an additional $50 per year in taxes.



Oakland school population

There were an estimated 197,489 students in the Oakland County schools at the start of the 2000-01 school year, a gain of more than 3,000 students since 1999. But not every school is gaining pupils, as families move toward select high-growth areas.
