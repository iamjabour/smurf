EMI, HitHive Launch Wireless Music Sharing Service

The agreement between EMI and HitHive means that a huge collection of music -- including tunes from the Beatles -- can be stored, managed and played on virtually any Web-compatible wireless device.

Archived hits from EMI Recorded Music -- ranging from the Beatles to Frank Sinatra to Garth Brooks -- will soon be playing on a wireless device near you.

EMI, the world's third largest recording company, has linked up with HitHive, Inc. , a wireless music network provider, to distribute "a vast amount" of recordings from EMI's music catalog in digital format -- making HitHive the first company to deploy a sharing platform approved by a major music group.

Loan and Borrow Tunes

Under the terms of the deal, consumers can share their favorite EMI tunes with people around the globe on a limited basis, using HitHive's proprietary loan and borrow feature, which abides by the rights of artists and copyright owners.

HitHive's controlled system allows users to invite friends to listen to their music through all types of wireless devices, including wireless phones and PDAs as well as PCs and other music playback devices, for a specified number of plays. Once they have listened to a shared track, they can buy the song or album and have it automatically loaded into their own collection.

HitHive said its software technology also offers the ability to store, manage and play music on virtually any Web-compatible wireless device. Listeners can rate songs, review cuts and trade opinions with other music fans via the HitHive Web site.

Making Money from Music

The agreement also grants HitHive the opportunity to offer more revenue-generating services for its business partners through music subscription services, music-based promotions, music samples, commerce in digital music, and streaming and downloading features, the company said.

"Bringing EMI's music to the wireless world will allow music fans to enjoy their favorite artists anytime, anywhere," said Jay Samit, senior vice president at EMI Recorded Music.

EMI Recorded Music is an international music company with a presence in some 70 countries worldwide. The company has 1,500 artists in every leading music genre, including pop, rock, jazz, classical, Latin, Christian, country, rap/urban and dance, and releases more than 1,000 albums each year. EMI Recorded Music labels include Capitol, Angel, Blue Note, EMI, Priority and Virgin.

HitHive, which claims to have created the world's first wireless music network with on-demand access, is deploying a global media network that lets businesses deliver digital music directly to consumers.
