Description

The U.S. Equity Value Fund is invested primarily in mid-sized U.S. equity issues which have been identified as being inexpensive on a cash flow basis. They also have the additional attraction of unique proprietary advantage within their industry.

The U.S. Equity Value Fund is considered foreign content in a tax sheltered plan.



Risks

Diversification and thorough internal research conducted by the U.S. equity portfolio management team are key fundamentals in the risk management process. Individual security/sector risk is controlled through diversification of holdings among at least 50 companies. Market risk is controlled further through a firm discipline of selling companies when they reach price targets.



Investment Strategy

Investments are made in companies that are able to protect and enhance their market share, sales, cash flow and earnings. The objective of the Fund is to outperform the S&P 500 by one per cent per year over a full market cycle.



Suitability

The Fund is suited for investors with a long-term outlook (minimum 5 to 10 years). It offers investors the ability to invest in the U.S. market. Historically, investors willing to accept the higher short-term risk in the stock market have been rewarded with higher long-term returns (compared to less risky investments).
