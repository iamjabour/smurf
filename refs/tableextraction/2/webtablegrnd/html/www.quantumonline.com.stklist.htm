Special Stock Lists

    This page will produce a variety of stock lists such as the stocks comprising stock market averages, lists of all preferred stocks, warrants, etc., lists of stocks by state or country, and lists like the NAIC Top 200, etc. If you are looking for ideas or just general information, these stock lists might be useful to you.

    The stock lists produced by the links given below will include the stock's ticker symbol, the name of the company and other appropriate information. By clicking on a company's ticker symbol on the list, you will produce a page giving the company's information from our database. To produce one of the lists, just click on the underlined heading of any of the lists described below:

    Special Stock Lists

    Stocks that have had recent IPO's. - The 1000 most recent Initial Public Offerings (IPO's). The list of IPO's includes essentially all 1999 IPO's, most of the 1998 IPO's and many 1997 & 1996 IPO's.

    Stocks on the Year 2000 NAIC Top 200 List - This is a list of the 200 most widely held stocks of investment clubs associated with the National Association of Investors Corporation (NAIC) . The rankings are based on the number of investment clubs holding the stock. For further information on NAIC and the Top 200 list, go to the NAIC's web site at www.better-investing.org .

    Stocks with Dividend Reinvestment (DRIP) Programs - A list of stocks providing online information on their Dividend Reinvestment (DRIP) Program. The listings include a direct link to the DRIP program description. Note: only stocks that offer online information on their DRIP program are included on the list. These links are still being accumulated on a low priority basis and the list currently contains only a few hundred companies with many more to be added.

    Stocks that provide Online signup for Emailed News Releases - A list of stocks that provide an online signup page for investors to sign up to receive the company's news releases via email immediately upon release. The listings include a direct link to the signup page. Note: only stocks that offer online signup for emailed news releases are included on the list. These links are still being accumulated on a low priority basis and the list currently contains only a few hundred companies with many more to be added.

    Special Stock Categories

    These lists include special categories of stocks including preferred stocks, warrants, Wall Street special products, exchange traded closed-end funds, and real estate investment trusts. The lists contain most stocks in the categories but should not be considered as all-inclusive.

    Preferred Stocks - This category includes regular preferred stocks plus those issued by the special capital trusts that many companies are currently establishing.

    Warrants - This category includes the warrants to purchase a company's stock under specific conditions as defined by the company. These special conditions should be carefully investigated before purchasing these securities and are often poorly defined online.

    Special Products - This category includes the many special products currently being issued by Wall Street. Examples of special products include ACES, BRIDGES, DECS, MIDS, MITTS, PEPS, PEEQS, PERQS, PRIDES, QUIBS, QUICS, QUIDS, QUIPS, SAILS, SPERs, STRYPES, TAPS, TRACES, WEBS, and YEELDS. We will leave it to the user of the list to figure out what the initials mean and what do do with that information.

    Exchange traded Closed-end Funds - This category includes the many exchange traded closed-end management investment companies (mutual funds) traded on the stock exchanges.

    Exchange traded Closed-end Index Funds - This category includes the exchange traded closed-end index funds traded on the stock exchanges. We are still accumulating and coding these index funds so the list is not complete but does show many of the exchange traded index funds. Also whole groups of funds are being created recently so the list is expanding constantly.

    Real Estate Investment Trusts (REIT) - This category includes the many real estate investment trusts traded on the stock exchanges.
