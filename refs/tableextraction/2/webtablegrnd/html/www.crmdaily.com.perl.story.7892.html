Sybase Closes Intelligence Value Chain Circle

Under the leadership of CEO John S. Chen, Sybase partnered with Informatica for data extraction from CRM, ERP, legacy and other external systems to achieve 'a complete intelligence value chain.'

On target with expectations, Sybase, Inc. (Nasdaq: SYBS) announced that it has completed the integration of its industry warehouse studio software with offerings from business intelligence market leaders Cognos, Business Objects and MicroStrategy, Inc. (Nasdaq: MSTR).

Sybase said this move completes its intelligent value chain management strategy, which kicked off last year with the company's announcement of a partnership with Informatica Corporation (Nasdaq: INFA).

Sybase's partnership with Informatica aimed to fulfill the data acquisition aspect of the company's intelligence value chain management strategy. The original equipment manufacturer agreement combined Informatica's integration software with Sybase's industry warehouse application and gave Sybase the right to resell it and other Informatica products.

Broad and Deep Analysis

The current integrations of Sybase's industry warehouse studio will encompass data acquisition, data modeling, metadata management, data analysis, data storage and analytic applications.

"One of the key differences in Sybase's approach is that the analysis is both broad and deep, so the level of integrations achieved is significant," said Jacqueline Sweeny-Coolidge, senior analyst for the Hurwitz Group, Inc. "At the same time, it is implemented on an open architecture, which lets customers utilize the data store and front-end tools of their choice."

Sweeny-Coolidge added that the "Sybase Business Intelligence infrastructure reduces risk and impact to an organization's existing business systems while providing them with a jumpstart toward competitive advantage."

Looking at All the Links

To reach what it calls "a complete intelligence value chain," Sybase has partnered with Informatica for data extraction from CRM, ERP, legacy and other external systems.

Sybase's industry warehouse studio and adaptive server IQ multiplex manage the extracted data in conjunction with data warehouses from various vendors.

Then, products from Cognos, Business Objects, MicroStrategy and Compudigm International allow the data to be mined, visualized and acted on.

Finally, analytic applications from EDS allow end users to interact with the data.

According to Eric Miles, general manager and senior vice president of Sybase's business intelligence division, "Our value chain reduces the time it takes to implement a business intelligence solution and produce actionable intelligence. A project that would normally take two years to develop can now be reduced to six months."
