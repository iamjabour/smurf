American Jewish World Service [AJWS] is an independent non-profit organization founded in 1985 to help alleviate poverty, hunger and disease among the people of the world regardless of race, religion or nationality. AJWS provides humanitarian aid, technical support, emergency relief and skilled volunteers to grassroots project partners that are implementing small-scale, self-sustaining development projects in the areas of healthcare, education, economic development and agriculture reform. AJWS breathes life into Judaism's imperative to pursue justice and helps American Jews act upon a deeply felt obligation to improve the quality of life for all people.

Emergency Relief - AJWS responds to humanitarian disasters around the world by providing desperately needed emergency relief, and helping to fund long-term reconstruction. Immediate emergency relief assistance often includes clean water, shelter, sanitation, medical supplies, professional staff and other necessary assistance.

AJWS is currently accepting contributions to aid the victims of the latest earthquakes in India and Central America.
