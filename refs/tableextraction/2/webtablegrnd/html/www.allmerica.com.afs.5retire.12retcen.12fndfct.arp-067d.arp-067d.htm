Investment Philosophy

Cramer Rosenthal McGlynn (CRM) manages portfolios with the belief that successful investing is a result of recognizing dynamic change in a particular company before its impact is fully recognized by other investors. The most attractive opportunities are found among those securities that have been out of favor, where Wall Street coverage is limited, and where there is a degree of misunderstanding or neglect resulting in low expectations. CRM employs rigorous analysis, with an emphasis on cash flow, to develop a reasonable value for each company. Comprehensive on-site due diligence is employed before investing in any company.

Commentary

The Select Value Opportunity Fund underperformed the Russell 2500 Value Index*, yet outperformed the Russell 2500 Index for the fourth quarter.

The implosion of the dot-com bubble, earnings disappointments, and declining demand put tremendous downward pressure on the technology sector. Value returned to favor once again, maintaining its position as the clear choice for investors throughout 2000.

The Fund benefited from strong performance in insurance, defense, health care, aerospace, energy/utility, and consumer-related companies. Insurance company, Everest Re (3.30% of portfolio) was again the Fund?s largest contributor and L3 Communications (2.90%), a defense electronics company, is attracting investor interest. New holdings include Alberta Energy (2.80%), a leading producer of natural gas, which is enjoying high prices as a result of the cold winter and supply shortage.

The unwinding of speculative excesses and overall economic uncertainty may continue to create stock volatility, resulting in numerous buying opportunities. Given renewed investor focus, the manager remains confident that value will continue to be realized going forward.
