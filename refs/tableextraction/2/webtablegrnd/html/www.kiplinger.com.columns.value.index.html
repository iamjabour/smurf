Energy Stocks That Haven't Run Out of Gas

Portfolio Strategy

The cyclicality of energy companies is notorious. When prices rise, as they have in the last year or so, their stock prices tend to soar. When they fall, their stocks do, too. Our best guess (see "Energy Bubble?" in the July issue) is that the stocks will stay under pressure for the remainder of the year.

But owning at least some energy stocks makes good sense to round out a portfolio. The more conservative companies named in the accompanying article by James Bianco are better bets for buy-and-hold investors.

During the past year, while tech crashed and many other stock sectors stalled, the oil and gas patch was cooking. For the past 12 months, the average energy fund gained about 30%.

Is the party over? Not according to James Bianco of the Leuthold Group, a Minneapolis-based research group. "Oil and gas groups are cheap," he says. "Refining margins are huge."

Still room for a run-up

The profits of integrated oil firms, as well as refining and marketing companies, don't depend directly on the price of oil at the wellhead or the price of gasoline. Instead, they rise and fall with refining margins -- how much they can charge for refining products.

Bianco says those margins are quite high and should remain so for some time.

And five of the seven stocks Bianco recommends have price-earnings ratios of less than ten, based on earnings for the previous 12 months.

They are Amerada Hess ( AHC ), Forest Oil ( FST ), Phillips Petroleum ( P ), Sunoco ( SUN ) and Valero Energy ( VLO ). Bianco also likes Texaco ( TX ), which has a P/E of 16, and Unocal ( UCL ), with a P/E of 12.

Demand for natural gas

Andy Pilara, manager of RS Global Natural Resources fund ( RSNRX , Web site ), is likewise bullish on energy.

For example, Pilara likes Allegheny Energy ( AYE ), a utility company with low-cost coal production. That may be a good bet because President Bush's energy plan encourages more use of coal.

Pilara also thinks natural gas stocks are a good buy. He argues that supply of natural gas is going to take a long time catching demand. Since it's still difficult to liquefy and ship natural gas from one continent to another, most has to be produced near home.

About 90% of new electrical capacity in the U.S. is designed for environmentally clean natural gas. As a result, even though the price of natural gas has risen more than fourfold in the last year, "We still see a supply that will not meet demand," Pilara explains.

Some of his stock picks are a lot less tame than Bianco's. Among natural gas stocks, Pilara likes EOG ( EOG ), an old spinoff from Enron, and Questar ( STR ).

Northern exposure

But Pilara says many Canadian firms are trading at 40% to 50% discounts to U.S. firms on a price-to-cash-flow basis.

Among his favorites north of the border are Anderson Exploration (AXN) and Pan Canadian Petroleum (PCP on the Toronto Exchange). While the Canadian dollar is currently worth only about 85 cents, Pilara believes the currency will strengthen, too.

Among oil stocks, Pilara likes U.S.-based Murphy Oil ( MUR ) and BJS Services ( BJS ) (see " Growing Up Fast ," our February cover story) but also drifts to Canada to find his cheapest stocks. Among them: Precision Drilling (PDS) and Ensign Resource Service Group (ESI on the Toronto Exchange).
