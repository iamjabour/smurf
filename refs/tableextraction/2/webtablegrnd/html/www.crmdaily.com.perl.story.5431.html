TeleTech Clears Newgen Results Acquisition Hurdle

November 20, 2000



The deal will enable TeleTech to expand its vertical market focus.



In This Story:



Setting a Trend



By The Numbers



CRM provider TeleTech (Nasdaq: TTEC) received regulatory approval for its proposed acquisition of Newgen Results (Nasdaq: NWGN), leaving only Newgen's shareholders to sanction the stock deal valued at US$200 million.

Shareholders will meet December 20th to vote on the proposal. TeleTech said its acquisition of the San Diego, California-based Newgen is expected to close immediately after the vote.

Setting a Trend

The deal, which was announced in August, has triggered a one-a-month acquisition rate for Denver, Colorado-based TeleTech.

Newgen Results provides CRM and business-to-business (B2B) channel management services to major automobile companies like Ford Motor Co., General Motors, Volkswagen, Saab, Volvo and others. The company supports a network of 5,000 dealerships across the United States, providing targeted marketing and customer retention applications.

TeleTech CEO Scott Thompson said at the time that the acquisition would enable his company to expand its vertical market focus to companies with extensive dealer relationships like pharmaceuticals, office automation and financial services.

Citing forecasts that claim that manufacturers expect resellers to be responsible for 84 percent of sales during the next two years, TeleTech said that it could enhance the dealer-manufacturer relationship and greatly improve its position in the indirect channel.

By The Numbers

Newgen Results booked revenues of $39.8 million and a net income of $4.7 million for the first six months of this year. TeleTech generated $213 million in revenues in the third quarter and a net income of $11.7 million.

The acquisition of Newgen Results was the first in a recent string of deals for TeleTech. In September, it acquired Contact Center Holding SA, one of Spain's largest privately held CRM providers, for $100 million in stock. In October, the company bought the customer care division of Boston Communications Group for $15 million, and last week it purchased Hong Kong-based iCcare Limited for $4 million.
