1998-99 EMMS Email User Poll

Thank you very much for participating in the 17th annual EMMS Messaging Users Poll. By the time the poll closed Feb. 26, we had received 8,232 votes in the four categories from 2,280 people in at least 44 countries.

Microsoft, IBM/Lotus and AOL/Netscape were the big winners, but in one respect email also was a big winner. By helping to make this year's poll such a success, you have once again proven in action that email is the "killer" application of the Internet.

For the first seven years of the EMMS poll (1982-1989), the winners were determined by a small blue ribbon panel of messaging industry insiders. Then in 1990, the process was opened up to all email users, a decision that has served us well ever since. In a way, the results are still determined by a group of industry insiders, except this year there were 2,280 of you.

About 1,633 votes came in from people using email addresses in the ".com" domain, and about 205 came in from the ".net" domain. Of the rest, approximately 338 came in from one of 44 different two-letter country domains, including 37 from Canada, 28 from Germany, 27 from Taiwan, and 20 each from China, Denmark and the United Kingdom.

A total of 640 people gave us permission to list their names in the March 5, 1999, issue of EMMS, and on this Web site. Thank you all for taking the time to participate. Thanks also for once again showing the world that when it comes to the Internet, the message is the medium (with apologies to Marshall McLuhan).
