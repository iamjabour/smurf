Oracle Results Lift Sagging Tech Fortunes

Software giant Oracle Corporation (Nasdaq: ORCL) put a little holiday cheer back into the sagging technology industry's Grinch-like season, boosting net income 62 percent over last year and picking up the pace with its e-business applications sales.

The Redwood Shores, California-based company, the second-largest software company in the world after Microsoft, booked a net income of US$623 million on sales of $2.7 billion. Oracle chief executive officer Larry Ellison said the results of the second quarter show that the company's new e-business suite has become a magnet for attracting new customers.

"All the applications in our suite are designed and engineered to work together, so customers buying the entire suite don't need to do any systems integration," Ellison said noting that this quarters sales reports indicate that Oracle's suite is winning over customers from competing firms.

Applications, Database Sales Up

Oracle said its applications software sales are up 66 percent and database sales up 19 percent. The company said that overall, it has doubled operating margins and more than doubled profits in just two years.

Among the company's new CRM customers for the quarter are Hewlett-Packard, Finisar Corporation, Simplex and SymmetriCom. New supply chain applications customers in the quarter included Qualcomm, Boeing, the UK Ministry of Defense and Entel. The company also attracted BankWest, GT Group Telecom and Seagate Technology as new enterprise resource planning (ERP) clients.

Overseas Market Up

Oracle offered its investors more good news with the report that its applications business in the Europe, Middle East and Africa regions is up by 91 percent for the quarter and that 500 companies and organizations in the region have opted for its e-business suite.

Among them, Oracle said, is the famed La Scala Theatre in Milan, Italy. La Scala, site of some of opera's most noteworthy performances, uses the applications to sell CD's and manage customer relations, Oracle said.

The company also said its eSpin joint initiative with Italian car maker Fiat and consultant Cap Gemini Ernst & Young is up and running, and is expected to deliver $250 million in sales in the "medium-term." Oracle has a 10 percent stake in eSpin, a B2B market exchange model that it says will reach break-even status in the next two years.

Spreading Holiday Cheer

Industry analysts breathed a palpable, if perhaps brief, sigh of relief Friday morning on hearing the Oracle results. Thursday, Microsoft cast a dark shadow on what has been a dismal season for technology companies by announcing that it expects earnings to be lower than projected.

Industry heavyweights like Apple Computer, Compaq, Intel, Motorola and 3Com have all issued earnings warnings for the quarter, while Internet companies like CMGI have stumbled badly and many online retailers are likely wrapping and shipping their last presents.
