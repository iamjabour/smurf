VERITY ANNOUNCES RECORD SECOND QUARTER RESULTS, REAFFIRMS PROJECTION OF 50%-PLUS REVENUE GROWTH FOR FISCAL 2001



SUNNYVALE, Calif. ? December 13, 2000 - Verity, Inc. (Nasdaq: VRTY), the company powering business portals, today reported record financial results for its fiscal quarter ended November 30, 2000, the second quarter of its fiscal year ended May 31, 2001, and issued projections for its operations during the remainder of the fiscal year. Verity's revenues for its second fiscal quarter were a record $34.5 million, representing a 107% increase over $16.7 million in revenues for the same period last year. Software product revenues, which represented 74% of total revenues for the quarter, were a record $25.6 million, which is a 153% increase over the same period last year.



For the six months ended November 30, 2000, total revenues were $65.8 million, representing an 80% increase over $36.5 million in revenues for the same period last year. Software product revenues for the six-month period ended November 30, 2000 were $48.4 million, which is a 97% increase over the same period last year.



The second quarter was a period of demonstrated market expansion for Verity. Verity?s European operations gained strength with new account growth and overall revenue share growth. Verity saw significant sales distributed across core markets, including Intranet portal and e-commerce solutions for major retail, telecommunications, banking and financial services, publishing, professional services, pharmaceutical, aerospace, insurance and high technology companies. The company also maintained sales to new media sites, government agencies and once again concluded OEM transactions with major technology vendors.



Net income for the quarter was $8.2 million, or $0.23 per diluted share. Beginning this fiscal year, Verity's financial results are being reported on a fully taxed basis. For the same period last year, Verity's pro-forma net income was $1.1 million, or $0.03 per share, on a comparable fully taxed basis. Net income on a comparable fully taxed basis increased 659% over the same period last year.



For the six months ended November 30, 2000, net income was $15.4 million, or $0.42 per share. For the same six-month period last year, Verity?s pro-forma net income was $4.3 million, or $0.13 per share, on a comparable fully taxed basis. Net income on a comparable fully taxed basis increased 256% over the same period last year.



"Verity's second quarter's performance produced another quarter of excellent results," said Gary J. Sbona, Verity's chairman and CEO. "It was our fourth straight quarter of record revenues, our best quarter ever for net income and earnings per share on a fully taxed basis, and our eleventh consecutive quarter of profitability. We strengthened our organization with a number of new hires in key positions, increasing our total worldwide team to 428 employees. Our business remains strong and on track, and our management metrics are performing to our operating plan. As we have indicated previously, we project 50%-plus revenue growth for fiscal 2001, and we will continue to strengthen our organization accordingly."



Business Outlook for Fiscal 2001

Verity also released the following projections concerning its future operations. Readers are cautioned that these projections are subject to a number of risks and uncertainties as explained more fully below, and actual results could be materially different.



For the remaining two quarters of FY 2001, Verity projects that it will continue to achieve financial results consistent with its annual operating plan. Verity projects revenues to increase 9% to 11% on a sequential quarterly basis, which translates to projected Q3 revenues between $37.6 and $38.3 million and projected Q4 revenues between $41.0 and $42.5 million.



Verity projects earnings per share, on a fully taxed/fully diluted basis, to be between $0.23 and $0.25 per share in Q3 and between $0.24 and $0.27 per share in Q4.



In total, Verity projects revenues for FY 2001 to be between $144.4 million and $146.6 million, which equates to a 50% to 53% increase from FY 2000 revenues of $96.1 million.



Verity projects net income for FY 2001 to be between $33.0 million and $34.8 million on a fully taxed basis. This equates to a 71% to 80% increase from FY 2000 net income of $19.3 million on a comparable fully taxed basis.



Verity projects total year earnings per share for FY 2001 to be between $0.89 and $0.94 per share on a fully taxed/fully diluted basis, compared to $0.55 per share on a comparable fully taxed/fully diluted basis for FY 2000.

About Verity

Verity is a leader in powering business portals. These include corporate portals used for sharing information within an enterprise, e-commerce portals for online selling and market exchange portals for B2B activities. Business portals provide personalized information to employees, partners, customers and suppliers.



Verity products are used by 48 of the Fortune 100 and by more than 1,000 corporations worldwide. The markets Verity serves are comprised of e-commerce merchants, e-businesses, market exchanges, government agencies, online service providers, Internet publishers and software developers. Verity customers include Adobe Systems, American Greetings, AT&T, BroadVision, CDNOW, Chapters, Cisco, CNET, Compaq, Dow Jones, Edgar Online, Ernst & Young, Fairmarket, Financial Times, Globe and Mail, Home Depot, IBM, Lotus, NewsEdge Corporation, SAP, Siemens, Snap.com, Sybase, Time New Media and Timex.
