N. Coast stocks slowly rebound

Published on Wednesday, September 2, 1998

© 1998 The Press Democrat

By Ted Appel

Staff Writer

Most North Coast stocks inched back up Tuesday, reclaiming some of the ground they lost in Monday's steep slide.

With one major exception: Advanced Fibre Communications. The Petaluma telecommunications equipment manufacturer's stock continued to drop Tuesday, falling another 16 percent to $6. AFC shares have been in a dramatic tumble since July 1, when the stock was trading at about $40.

Investors tentatively resumed buying stock in other local companies, pushing the North Coast Stock Index of 20 companies up 2.4 percent Tuesday. Overall, North Coast shares regained about half of the losses sustained during Monday's meltdown in stock prices.

However, North Coast stocks did not bounce back as strong as Wall Street's two most widely watched yardsticks. The Dow Jones industrial average rose 3.8 percent percent on Tuesday while the Standard & Poor's 500 climbed 3.9 percent, regaining more than half of their losses on Monday.

The NCSI -- developed by the Sonoma State University Center for Regional Economics and The Press Democrat -- tracks the weekly change in the value of a portfolio comprised of a single share of stock in 20 companies on the North Coast.

On Tuesday, that hypothetical NCSI portfolio was worth $425.97, up from $415.90 on Monday but still down from $548.25 at the beginning of the year.

A recap:

Three stocks rose faster than the Dow and the S&P:

* Robert Mondavi stock jumped 11 percent, up $2.25 a share to $23.13.

* Westamerica Bank stock climbed 7 percent, up $1.81 a share to $26.06.

* DSC Communications stock increased 6 percent, up $1.56 a share to $25.69.

Two stocks kept pace with the Dow and the S&P:

* Optical Coating Laboratory stock rose 4 percent, up 63 cents a share to $16.38.

* Sola International stock grew 4 percent, up 56 cents a share to $15.06.

Eight stocks increased, but not as fast as the Dow and the S&P:

* Beringer Wine Estates stock increased almost 4 percent, up $1.13 per share to $31.63.

* Vacu-dry stock rose 3 percent, up 25 cents a share to $7.50.

* Hewlett-Packard stock rose 3 percent, up $1.57 a share to $50.13.

* Northern Empire Bancshares, the holding company for Sonoma National Bank, saw its stock increase 3 percent, up 75 cents to $27.50.

* Louisiana-Pacific stock grew 1 percent, up 25 cents a share to $19.

* Autodesk stock rose 1 percent, up 25 cents a share to $23.63

* Arterial Vascular Engineering stock increased 1 percent, up 31 cents a share to $35.31.

* Redwood Empire Bancorp, the parent company of National Bank of the Redwoods, saw its stock increase 1 percent, up 13 cents per share to close at $15.38.

Five stocks did not change:

* Broderbund Software stock held steady, closing at $13.94.

* Real Goods Trading Corp. stock stood still, closing at $3.88 a share.

* Tegal stock held steady, closing at $3.

* Mendocino Brewing stock stood still, closing at $1.25.

* Healthy Planet Products stock held steady, closing at $1 a share.

Two stocks fell:

* Advanced Fibre Communications stock tumbled another 16 percent, down $1.13 a share to $6.

* Exchange Bank stock slipped 0.3 percent, down 25 cents a share to $80.50.
