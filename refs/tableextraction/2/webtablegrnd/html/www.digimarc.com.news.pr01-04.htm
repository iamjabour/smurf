Digimarc Corporation Reports Record Fourth Quarter and Year-End Results

PORTLAND, Ore., February 7, 2001 - Digimarc Corporation (NASDAQ: DMRC), the world leader in digital watermark technology and applications, today announced record revenues of $3.4 million for its fourth quarter ended December 31, 2000.

Revenues for the fourth quarter of 2000 were $3.4 million, resulting in a 25 percent increase over the $2.7 million of revenues in the comparable period of 1999. Net loss per share for the fourth quarter of 2000 was $(0.19) based on 15.54 million weighted average basic and diluted shares outstanding for the quarter which was significantly smaller than the net loss per share of $(0.30) based on 5.14 million weighted average basic and diluted shares outstanding for the comparable period of 1999.

Revenues for the year ending December 31, 2000 were a record $11.9 million, resulting in a 71 percent increase over the $6.9 million of revenues for the year ending December 31, 1999. Net loss per share for the year ending December 31, 2000 was $(1.17) based on 13.57 million weighted average basic and diluted shares outstanding for the year, compared to a net loss per share of $(0.78) based on 3.05 million weighted average basic and diluted shares for the year ending December 31, 1999.

"In Q4 we continued progress toward our goal of making our technology a standard feature of all media content. The combination of continuing revenue growth and effective management of expenses contributed to strong earnings per share performance that exceeded analysts' consensus," said Bruce Davis, Digimarc's president and CEO. "Our platform strategy is focused on creating and fostering the deployment of valuable applications with powerful customers and other business partners that will drive ubiquitous adoption of digital watermarking."

"Highlights of year-end include the announcement of a $60 million strategic investment from industry leaders Philips and Macrovision, a joint venture with Philips to develop and market new video and audio applications, and new media commerce partnerships with Alchemedia and Replacements.com. We also received seven new U.S. patents and filed 120 new U.S. applications which brings our portfolio up to 22 U.S. patents granted and 176 pending," Davis said.

Davis continued, "In late 2000, we introduced Digimarc MediaBridge to the commercial printing market and began generating our first revenues from this segment. Intelligencer, our first licensee, is one of the largest direct-mail printers in the country. Also, Printer, Buyers and Brokerage Association International, the largest printing industry association, has signed a reseller agreement that targets small and mid size printers. Although we saw fewer Digimarc MediaBridge marked magazine ads than initially anticipated, Internet-enabled ads have appeared in numerous national publications including Wired, Popular Mechanics, Smart Money, Good Housekeeping, Entertainment Weekly, Seventeen and Expedia Travel."

"In 2000, our Secure Documents division made further inroads into the government sector. Digimarc has partnered with Logicon to offer Digimarc's ID mark solution as part of their Smart Card proposals to leading U.S. government departments and agencies and Emergent Information Technologies is adapting our technology for use by intelligence and defense agencies." About Digimarc

Digimarc Corp., based in Tualatin, Ore., (NASDAQ: DMRC) is the world leader in digital watermark technology and applications. Digimarc is creating an inherent, persistent digital identity for all media content through imperceptible digital code that can be embedded in any analog or digital material. The company has established three business units to focus on diverse media applications. Digimarc Media Commerce watermarking solutions enable protection and tracking, and also facilitate e-commerce of digital images, audio, and video. Digimarc Secure Documents provides anti-counterfeiting solutions to deter unauthorized PC-based reproduction of valuable documents such as financial, identification and product packaging. Digimarc MediaBridge - a fundamentally new way for consumers to access the Internet - allows printed materials to become direct portals to relevant destinations on the Internet. Digimarc's vision is to become a standard feature of all media content.
