Business Day (Johannesburg)

April 5, 2001

> Posted to the web April 5, 2001

Lukanyo Mnyanda

> Johannesburg

South Africa bond yields declined more than 20 basis points yesterday after government said it would probably cut its borrowing requirements for the current fiscal year.

Bonds reacted almost immediately and the yield on the government R150 long bond had dropped 23 basis points, or 1,95%, to 11,54% last night, from the previous day's close of 11,77%. It had gone as high as 11,83% in volatile trade on Tuesday.

The rand also recorded strong gains yesterday, helped by the dollar losing some ground after sharp losses on the US stock market on Tuesday. Equities, which had started the day lower, also rode on the new optimism and ended mostly flat.

Bond dealers said that the revised fiscal estimates had revived concern about the availability of paper on the market.

Kimon Boyiatjis, head of capital markets at Franklin Templeton NIB, said government's borrowing needs this year could be reduced by as much as R5bn.
