AT&T Wireless Hikes Bond Sale to $6.5B

March 1, 2001



With chairman and CEO John Zeglis in charge, AT&T Wireless is mounting what could be the largest private bond sale by a U.S. company.



In This Story:



Testing Wall Street



Plenty of Bonds -- Already



Related Stories



AT&T Wireless (NYSE: AWE) plans to sell US$6.5 billion in bonds to fuel expansion measures, according to industry reports.

AT&T Wireless is likely to sell the bonds at a 63 percent increase from the $4 billion to $5 billion in debt it expected to incur in what could be the largest ever private bond sale. The private sale allows AT&T Wireless to move more quickly than if it had to register in advance with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission for a public sale.

Last July, parent company AT&T Corp. (NYSE: T) was the first to make history with the largest private bond sale by a U.S. company when it sold $6 billion in bonds.

This news comes just weeks after AT&T Corp. said it will sell up to $3 billion of AT&T Wireless stock after the spin-off -- rather than before -- as a debt reduction measure.

Testing Wall Street

According to published reports, the AT&T Wireless debt sale will include $1 billion in 5-year notes yielding about 7.45 percent; $2.5 billion in 10-year notes yielding about 8.06 percent; and $2.5 billion in 30-year bonds yielding 3.4 percentage points more than benchmark Treasuries.

The 5- and 10-year notes will yield 2.75 percentage points and 3.1 percentage points, respectively, more than Treasuries with similar maturities.

AT&T Wireless plans to sell another $500 million in debt, according to reports, with a maturity yet to be determined. In any case, the reported debt sale will serve as a measure of investors' interest in high-yielding debt from wireless carriers.

Published reports indicate credit-rating agency Moody's Investors Service rated the company's debt at "Baa2," its second-lowest investment grade. Standard & Poor's, according to reports, rates the debt at "BBB," which is nearly the equivalent on its scale.

Merrill Lynch & Co. and Salomon Smith Barney reportedly are arranging the AT&T Wireless debt sale.

Plenty of Bonds -- Already

Despite more than fair yields on the notes, this may not be the best time for AT&T Wireless to be unloading so much debt.

According to industry reports, a plethora of bond sales -- particularly by telecommunications companies -- may have set the wrong stage for AT&T Wireless.

Published reports cite research from Newark, New Jersey-based Thomson Financial Securities Data that shows companies have sold $126.4 billion in investment-grade bonds already this year in the U.S. markets. Investors expect France Telecom to sell up to $8 billion more as early as next week.

Other reports said that J.P. Morgan Chase & Co. and Bank of America will arrange $4 billion in additional credit lines for AT&T Wireless.

Redmond, Washington-based AT&T Wireless is expected to be separated from the parent company later this year.
