Pens Score Player-Of-Month Hat Trick

Jagr Is 3rd Straight Pittsburgh Winner

PITTSBURGH, 10:48 a.m. EDT April 3, 2001 -- Jaromir Jagr is the third consecutive Penguins player to receive the NHL's Player of the Month award.

Jagr secured the honor for March with 15 goals and 16 assists. He follows previous winners Mario Lemieux and Alexei Kovalev.

Jagr had nine multiple-point efforts in eleven games.

March came in like a lion for the right winger, who was awarded points in six consecutive games, including a four-point performance against the Rangers on March 2.

Just eight days later, Jagr recorded his third hat trick of the season against the Calgary Flames.

But Lemieux could easily have taken the award, with 12 goals and 16 assists in 14 games.

Lemieux is averaging 1.73 points per game. That is the highest league average since the 1995-1996 season.
