Best Sports Figure



Deirdre Hamill/The Arizona Republic

He should be a superhero: Diamondbacks outfielder Luis Gonzalez is unfailingly solicitous of fans, charitable and quite the ballplayer, too.

The Arizona Republic

Sept. 17, 2000

Luis Gonzalez

Arizona Diamondbacks

Gonzo - the Diamondbacks' outfielder, not the purple thing with a beak - is everything John Rocker wishes he could be: a classy role model who is genuinely friendly and highly respected.

He made his debut with the team last year and set career highs in several categories, including homers (26), RBIs (111) and doubles (45), all while maintaining a professional, kid-friendly attitude. When the '99 season ended, he could have easily "tested the market" and joined another team for more money, but he chose to stay here, signing a three-year contract. Off the field, the bilingual star has been involved with several charitable causes, and he even plays congas in Big League Rocks, a CD that benefits the Major League Baseball Players Trust for Children.

And here's the clincher: He always treats adoring fans with the utmost respect.

Going, going …Gonzo!
