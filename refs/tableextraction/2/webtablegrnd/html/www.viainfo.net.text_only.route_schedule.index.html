Welcome to VIA’s route and schedule page, where riding the bus is made easy and convenient. Whether for work or play, VIA can take you almost anywhere in the San Antonio, Texas area. VIA operates 106 bus lines, serving an area of 1,233.78 square miles or 99% of Bexar County. Bus lines are divided into six categories radial, limited stop, express, crosstown, circulator, and streetcar.

Frequent asked questions concerning bus riding

Automated information

  • Radial - provides local service to the central business district and travels along most major streets. Bus lines 1 through 99 with the exception of lines listed below as limited stop and express are considered radial lines.
  • Limited stop - used primarily to reduce loads on local service and provide shorter travel time for longer trips. These lines follow the same route as a local line service these but only stop at high activity or transfer bus stops. Presently VIA operates twelve limited stop routes (3, 10, 21, 38, 31, 52, 76, 81, 87, 91, 550, and 551).
  • Express - provides non-stop service between major transit generators, park and ride facilities, and transfer centers. In order to provide customers the shortest travel time, the majority of the express line is operated on expressways. Presently VIA operates five express routes (17, 64, 65, 93, and 94).
  • Crosstown - provides service on major travel corridors that are outside the central business district. Crosstown lines generally intersect radial lines and optimize transfer connections. All 500 series routes are crosstowns with the exception of the 550 and 551.
  • Circulator - provide local circulation within residential areas and allow transfer connections with other lines at transfer centers or park and ride facilities. Circulator lines are numbered in the 600 series.
  • Streetcar - provide circulator service within the central business district with high frequency service levels. Presently VIA operates five streetcar lines (301-305), each line is distinguished by a color and symbol: route 301- red square, 302 – yellow star, 303 – purple diamond, 304 – brown triangle, and 305 – blue circle.

Route and schedule modifications are made three times a year to adjust to changing ridership patterns and area developments. Public hearings may be held to discuss these modifications. Holiday schedules are reduced to Saturday or Sunday service level to reflect ridership. Promotions and information for these modifications, public hearings and holiday schedules is accomplished using bus flyers, farebox cards, Press releases and updated pocket schedules.

Automated Information

VIAINFO, (210) 362-2020, is an automated information system that is available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. Bus schedule information can be accessed with a touch-tone phone by entering the four-digit and one letter bus stop number that appears on each bus stop sign and follow the instructions.

Need help planning your trip?

VIA’s friendly customer service representatives can help plan your bus trip. We will recommend the most suitable route and departure time, quote the fare, and advise you of any transfers you’ll need along the way.

Helpful tips when calling:

  • Have pencil and paper ready
  • Be ready to tell the operator

    where you are (nearest intersection)

    where you are going (nearest intersection)

    when you would like to get there

Our representatives are available Monday - Friday from 6 a.m. to 8 p.m., Saturday 7 a.m. to 7 p.m., and Sunday from 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Central Standard Time at (210) 362-2020 or TDD (210) 362-2019.
