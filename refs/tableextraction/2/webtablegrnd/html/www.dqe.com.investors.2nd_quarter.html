DQE 2nd Quarter Results Include Final Auction Filing; Announces gain expected in 3rd Quarter

PITTSBURGH, PA, August 4, 2000 – Duquesne Light, a DQE subsidiary, today submitted its Final Auction Accounting Filing with the Pennsylvania Public Utility Commission seeking approval for the accounting treatment of Duquesne's generation sale. The customer benefit of the proceeds from the generation sale will eliminate from customers' bills more than $1.2 billion of combined stranded costs and tax charges from the original stranded cost determination. As a result of this filing, the final collection of the Competitive Transition Charge is expected to occur in early 2002 for most major rate classes, thus achieving the shortest stranded cost recovery period of any major utility in the state and one of the shortest in the country. Residential stranded cost recovery is estimated to occur in February 2002, thereby resulting in one of the largest rate reductions in the state's history of at least 20% for residential customers.



DQE's second quarter comprehensive earnings for 2000 were reported at $.23 per share compared to $.56 in 1999, impacted by a $.16 per share decrease in stranded cost earnings compared with 1999, and two one-time charges totaling $11 million, or an additional $.16 per share. Approximately half the amount of the one-time charges related to the termination of a coal bed methane project, and the remaining amount related to the write-off due to various purchase accounting issues at AquaSource. The quarter's results also include the cost associated with the generation business' operations as well as associated support and administrative costs. With the sale of Duquesne's generating plants, the Company is consolidating administrative resources and has announced the reduction of approximately 230 people in its support and administrative workforce as part of streamlining its processes. These and other reductions are expected to result in a total cost savings of approximately $70 million on an annual basis and are expected to be completed by the fourth quarter of this year.

As part of the Company's exit from the generation business, Duquesne Energy, a DQE subsidiary, entered into a definitive agreement to sell its alternative fuel facilities. The facilities produce a coal based alternative fuel used in both manufacturing and generating facilities. A gain of approximately 85 cents per share would be recorded in the third quarter, contingent upon satisfaction of closing conditions.



The Company's 5 to 8 percent targeted annual growth in comprehensive earnings will be recognized through a combination of sustainable earnings in its delivery businesses, streamlined administrative infrastructure, increased market valuations of its investments and selective asset sales.



The Company will discuss the second quarter results as well as new developments in its businesses on Monday, August 7, 2000 at 3:00 p.m. EDT. The presentation and conference remarks can be accessed via the Internet at www.dqe.com.



The foregoing contains forward-looking statements regarding the following: Duquesne Light's final auction filing and the expected stranded cost recovery period and customer rate and bill reductions included therein; DQE's anticipated cost savings through its administrative workforce reductions; the sale of alternative fuel facilities and the results anticipated from such sale; and DQE's targeted annual growth rate of comprehensive earnings. Actual results may materially differ from those implied by such statements due to known and unknown risks, including but not limited to, the following: the results of Duquesne Light's final auction filing will depend on the ultimate determinations by the Pennsylvania Public Utility Commission; DQE's ability to centralize and streamline administrative functions, and to retrain and redeploy administrative employees, will affect the ultimate amount of cost reductions; the sale of alternative fuel facilities and all anticipated results are dependent on the satisfaction of closing conditions, including but not limited to, approval from the Federal Trade Commission and the Department of Justice on antitrust matter; and changing market conditions, weather conditions and demand for utility services could affect projected comprehensive earnings growth.
