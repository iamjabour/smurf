Wild Oats 1999 Q1 Results

BOULDER, CO - Wild Oats Markets, Inc. ("Wild Oats" or the "Company") today reported operating results for its first fiscal quarter ended April 3, 1999. The Company also announced the acquisition, in two separate transactions, of 11 natural foods markets.

Sales for the first quarter of 1999 were $122.5 million, an increase of 34% over sales of $91.6 million for the same period in 1998. The sales increase reflects the opening of one new store in Evanston, Illinois, the acquisition of three stores in Tucson, Arizona and the relocation of two stores in Tempe, Arizona and Memphis, Tennessee in the first quarter. Comparable store sales increased 8% for the quarter, reflecting positive comparable store sales in the Boulder market, better than expected performance in several relocated stores and the inclusion of Easter in the first quarter in 1999 versus the second quarter in 1998.

Net income for the first quarter of 1999 increased 17% to $3.2 million, exclusive of non-recurring expenses, from $2.7 million in the same period in 1998, and diluted earnings per share for the first quarter of 1999 increased 20% to $0.24, exclusive of non-recurring expenses, from $0.20 per share in the same period in 1998. The non-recurring expenses incurred in the first quarter related to Company decisions to cease operations at its two ?Farm to Market? concept stores, and redirect its efforts from managing certain vacant sites to focus on its continuing operations, recent acquisitions and new site development. These decisions resulted in a one-time charge to earnings of $10.9 million in the first quarter of 1999 comprised of the write-down of fixed assets, intangible assets and lease obligations for the selected sites.

Commenting on the Company's performance, CEO Mike Gilliland said, "We are very pleased with the Company's better than expected sales and operating performance in the first quarter of 1999. Our sales trends are very strong, we continue to generate record sales in our new store openings, and we have a full calendar of excellent new sites for the remainder of 1999 and 2000. The great success of our new People?s Market concept store in Evanston, Illinois and the significant expansion of our 1999 growth plans through our latest acquisitions has caused us to refocus our efforts on the Company?s new opportunities and future growth."

Wild Oats has signed an agreement to acquire from United Natural Foods, Inc., in a cash-for-assets transaction scheduled to close on April 30, 1999, three natural foods markets in Westport and Hartford, Connecticut, and Melbourne, Florida in exchange for $6.5 million in cash. The stores currently operate under the names ?Food for Thought?, ?Cheese and Stuff? and ?Natureworks!? and are 12,000, 10,000 and 11,200 square feet, respectively. The Company will relocate the Westport store to its new site in Westport scheduled to open in the fourth quarter of 1999.

Wild Oats has signed an agreement to acquire from General Nutrition Incorporated ("GNC"), in a cash-for-stock transaction scheduled to close on May 29, 1999, all of the stock of Nature's Fresh Northwest ("Nature's") in exchange for $40 million in cash plus the assumption of a $17 million note payable from Nature?s to GNC. Nature's currently operates six stores in Portland, Oregon and Vancouver, Washington, ranging in size from 8,300 to 42,000 square feet, which operate under the names "Nature's Fresh Northwest," "Nature?s Marketplace" and "Nature's Northwest." In addition, there are two new Nature's sites and one relocation under development, all of which are expected to be completed before the end of 1999 and which are included in the acquisition. The stores will continue to operate under the various Nature?s names.

Commenting on the acquisitions, CEO Mike Gilliland said, "These two acquisitions will nearly double the Company"s new store plan for 1999, and will add approximately $100 million in incremental revenue in the year 2000. Both allow us to enter strategically important new markets. The Connecticut stores will complement the performance of our new sites in West Hartford and Westport scheduled to open in the fourth quarter of 1999. The Nature's stores have a very strong presence and brand equity in Portland, and will serve as an excellent launching point for further new store development in the Pacific Northwest."
