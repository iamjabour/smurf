May 24- Desert Heat Claims Lives

Twelve Mexican citizens died Wednesday in the desert of Arizona when they were overcome by extreme temperatures approaching 110° F. All of the victims were discovered by Border Patrol Agents in the San Cristobal Valley. Rescuers are continuing to search for four persons who are now missing. The group had been attempting to illegally enter the United States through the rugged region.

May 24- Cyclone Trolls Off Coast Of India

India's Meteorology Department is warning residents along the western coast of the country to keep an eye on a developing cyclone. The cyclone (01A) is now packing winds in excess of 100 mph. The storm is likely to intensify and could threaten coastal areas in the next few days.

May 23- Record Heat Scorches Pacific NW

Several temperature records fell yesterday across the Pacific Northwest as favorable winds combined with high pressure to push the thermometer near the century mark. Tuesday's records included 100° F in Medford, Oregon, 95° in Portland, Oregon and 90° F at Mount Shasta, California. Cooler air from the ocean should arrive today.

May 23- Java Landslide Leaves Nine Dead

Pouring rain ignited a fast moving landslide that buried at least six homes in a small village in western Java. Media reports indicate that nine people died and six were injured. Rescuers continue to look for missing villagers buried under mud and debris.

May 23- Severe Storms Strike Mid Atlantic US

Powerful thunderstorms rolled across the Mid Atlantic region yesterday causing flash floods and power outages. At least one tornado was reported south of Washington D.C. A two year old boy in West Virginia died in the storms when he fell into a rain swollen river.

May 22- Russian Authorities Monitor River

Russian emergency officials are keeping a careful eye on rising water levels as the Lena continues to swell. The rampaging river now threatens to inundate the city of Yakutsk. Reportedly more than 200,000 people are at risk. Residents are scrambling to build barriers before the water crests later this week. Recent flooding in Siberia has claimed at least two lives.

May 22- NOAA Predicts Normal Storm Season

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration is predicting a normal Atlantic tropical storm season this summer with a total of 5 to 7 hurricanes by the end of November. At a news conference held in Washington D.C. forecasters noted the absence of La Nina conditions this year will likely result in a season with fewer storms than in the recent past. NOAA plans an updated hurricane forecast outlook in August.

May 21- NASA Awards Imager Contract

The National Air and Space Administration (NASA) has granted an $8 million dollar contract with ITT Industries to create the next generation of space weather imagers. The project which will be funded by NOAA should launch by 2008. The new imager which will view the Earth from a 22,300-mile orbit over the equator should dramatically improve the forecasting of severe weather including hurricanes and tornadoes.

May 20- Flooding Rain Leaves Ruin In Congo

Two days of heavy rain in Congo's capital of Kinshasa has left at least 30 people dead. Many of the victims were reportedly buried by mud and debris. Rescue workers continue to look for several dozen missing persons. Climatologically Congo often experiences short bursts of intense rain during the rainy month of May.

May 18- Bush Energy Plan Criticized

Calling the new energy plan unveiled by the Bush administration "a disastrous development'', U.N. Climate chief Jan Pronk told a Dutch television station he believes the plan will lead to increased output of carbon dioxide. In March, the United States rejected the Kyoto Accord angering many European leaders concerned about the threat of global warming. The United States has agreed to a new round of climate pact discussions in July.

May 17- Spring Drought Worries Easterners

With lawns turning brown and stream flows ebbing many residents of the eastern seaboard are wondering where is the rainwater. Precipitation for the three weeks ending yesterday totaled zero from New York to Virginia. Many towns are now considering restrictions to curtail water use. Rainfall totals are now running 3 to 7 inches below normal across the region for 2001.

May 17- "Epic" Floods Strike Russia

Hot weather and melting snow have combined to create a messy situation in eastern Siberia where several large rivers including the Lena have burst their banks. Russian authorities have evacuated hundreds of families as floodwaters raced through riverside towns. Explosives have been used to dislodge ice jams which have contributed to the rising water.
