Market Outlook
Are We There Yet?

By: Adam Martin

After a lower opening, stocks have moved strongly into positive territory this morning. Yesterday's announcement of a half point rate cut left the markets yawning, with more expected today, and this morning's surge is a pleasant surprise. Fed Chairman Alan Greenspan said yesterday that inflation is not as much of an issue as the threat of recession, a position reinforced by the CPI coming in lower than expected. Earnings reports released last night were a mixed bag of hits and misses, and many traders still feel that April's rally may have overdone itself. Some analysts feel the market will continue to slip until at least midyear, with little optimism for good earnings news for the second quarter. All of this adds up to a high likelihood of a sluggish market as we've seen for much of the month.
