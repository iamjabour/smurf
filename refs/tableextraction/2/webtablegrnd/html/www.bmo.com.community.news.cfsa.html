Canadian Figure Skating Championships

Bank of Montreal is the title sponsor of Canada's most prestigious figure skating event, the BMO Canadian Championships being held January 18 through 21 in Winnipeg, Manitoba, at the Winnipeg Arena.



As title sponsor for the fifth consecutive year, Bank of Montreal has taken on the role of primary supporter for Canada's premier figure skating competition. Canadian figure skaters are among the best in the world and Bank of Montreal, working in concert with the Skate Canada (CFSA) and its other corporate partners, is committed to making this year's event a fitting showcase for these extraordinary athletes. Many of the competitors will go on to the World Championships in March 2001 in Vancouver, Canada.

Figure Skating For Canadians Of All Ages - And All Walks Of Life



While sponsoring the Canadian Championships is important to Canada's continuing excellence in figure skating, it is only the most visible component of Bank of Montreal's deep, long-term commitment to this popular and graceful sport.



Competitions



In addition to the Canadian Championships, Bank of Montreal has taken on the role of title sponsor for the BMO Skate Canada Eastern and Western Challenges, the qualifying events that lead to the Canadian Championships, which are held in major regional centres each December. More than 1,000 skaters compete at the two regional events.



The bank also sponsors the BMO Skate Canada Sectionals. The 13 Sectional Skating Championships (one for each province, except Ontario, which due to its population, has four) are held each November in cities across Canada and determine which skaters advance to the Eastern and Western Challenges.



Bank of Montreal has also added the BMO Skate Canada Synchronized Nationals to its portfolio of Skate Canada sponsorships. The 2001 Championships will be held in Hull, Quebec from March 1-4, with some 80 teams expected to take to the ice during the four-day competition.



Instruction and Skills Development



Bank of Montreal is also the sponsor of the Skate Canada's Learn-To-Skate programs, where skaters of all ages participate in both recreational and competitive skating. Through this program, Bank of Montreal supports the development of young skaters at Skate Canada clubs across the country. Over the next few years, the Bank will provide even more "grass roots" support for figure skating by strengthening its employee and branch participation in local figure skating initiatives across the country.

Skate Canada and Canada's National Team

Bank of Montreal is a major sponsor of the Canadian National Figure Skating Team, and of Skate Canada, the governing body of Canadian figure skating that oversees more than 1450 clubs and nearly 200,000 members. Through this support, Bank of Montreal has made a major, long-term commitment to promoting Canadian figure skating excellence.

Our Employees Lend a Hand

Since announcing its sponsorship of the Canadian Championships in October 1996, the bank has encouraged employees across the country to participate in the national figure skating program. To date, each of the bank's 32,000 employees has received educational materials on figure skating, to help them assist figure skating in their own communities.

In turn, Bank of Montreal employees have responded with an impressive show of support. This year, several hundred bank employees will work as volunteers at the Canadian Championships and other skating related projects.

Why Does the Bank Support Figure Skating?

Figure skating enjoys a special cultural significance to Canadians and Bank of Montreal is proud to support this enduring national pastime. In a country noted for its regional diversity, figure skating is a common denominator. More than six million Canadians - almost 25% of the national population - are involved in this aesthetically graceful sport. Today, interest in figure skating is at an all-time high in Canada. This year's Canadian Championships are forecast to be one of the most competitive and, by far, the most widely-watched ever.



Activities at the Winnipeg Arena

In addition to the spectacle on the ice, there is also plenty to take in away from the rink. Here's a quick preview of what to see and do throughout the four-day event.

Bank of Montreal Centre: Bank of Montreal has created an interactive centre that will serve as the focal point for off-ice activities during the Canadian Championships. Fans can trade their favourite figure skating pins, sign a special banner that will be presented to the Canadian Figure Skating Team competing in the World Championships in Vancouver, British Colombia, or explore the "Road to the Canadians", which describes the odyssey from club competition to the Bank of Montreal Canadian Championships. On Saturday, January 20, all ticket holders are invited to the Bank of Montreal Centre to obtain autographs from members of the Canadian National Skating Team.

Bank of Montreal Parents Lounge: Bank of Montreal is providing a private lounge for the parents of athletes who have travelled across the country to watch their son or daughter compete.
