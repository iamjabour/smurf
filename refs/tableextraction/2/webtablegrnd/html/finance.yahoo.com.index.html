Top Business News as of 2:46pm ET
Stocks Up, Buyers Bet on Economic Rebound

Stocks headed higher in mid-afternoon trading on Wednesday, lifting the blue-chip Dow further above the key 11,000 level, as Wall Street bet the Federal Reserve's string of five large interest-rate cuts would fuel an economic rebound this year.

Inflation Milder Than Expected in April

U.S. consumer prices rose more moderately than expected in April, helping to give the Federal Reserve some leeway in its aggressive interest-rate cutting campaign.

Krispy Kreme Earnings Hot As Doughnuts

Krispy Kreme Doughnuts Inc. on Wednesday reported an earnings increase as hot as its trademark glazed doughnuts, up nearly 89 percent in the fiscal first quarter, and said the future looks bright for the rapidly expanding chain.

Tobacco Cos Found Not Guilty in N.J. Case

Philip Morris Cos. Inc. and R.J. Reynolds Tobacco Holdings Inc., the two largest U.S. tobacco companies, were found not guilty on Wednesday in a case brought by the husband of a deceased former smoker.

American Home Products Sees Earns in Line

Drug maker American Home Products Corp. on Wednesday projected earnings in 2001 roughly in line with analysts' expectations and said it expects sales of its antidepressant Effexor medicine to reach $2 billion by 2002.

Campbell Soup Earnings Fall, Meet View

Campbell Soup Co., the world's biggest soup maker, said on Wednesday its fiscal third-quarter earnings fell 12 percent, meeting estimates, as results were hurt in part by higher marketing expenses.

Sprint Falls After Latest Profit Warning

Shares of Sprint Corp. fell 2.2 percent on Wednesday after the No. 3 U.S. long-distance telephone company cut its 2001 growth outlook for the third time this year, citing some cash-strapped wholesale customers that failed to pay their bills.

Pfizer, Merck Jump After New Guidelines

Merck & Co. and Pfizer Inc. led drug shares higher on Wednesday as new federal guidelines are expected to spur sales growth of their highly profitable anti-cholesterol drugs, analysts said.

Homebuilder Stocks Fall on Report

U.S. homebuilder stocks fell on Wednesday after Salomon Smith Barney lowered its investment rating on some of the builders and said national housing data are likely to weaken by the middle of the year.

Rite Aid Sets $3 Billion Refinancing Package

No. 3 U.S. drugstore chain Rite Aid Corp., which is trying to recover from a failed expansion plan and two years of losses, said on Wednesday it expects by August to secure $3 billion in refinancing and credit it can tap to anchor its turnaround.
