MTI Reports Fiscal 1998 Results

MTI Reports Revenues of $200 Million for Fiscal 1998, up 30% Over Prior Year

ANAHEIM, Calif., May 21, 1998 -- MTI Technology Corp. (Nasdaq:MTIC) today reported net income for the fourth quarter of fiscal 1998 ended April 4, 1998 of $5.8 million, or 19 cents diluted earnings per share, compared to net income of $2.2 million, or 8 cents diluted earnings per share, in the same quarter of the previous year, and $5.1 million, or 17 cents diluted earnings per share for the immediately preceding quarter. Total revenue for the fourth quarter rose 32% to $55.5 million, compared to $42.1 million for last year?s fourth quarter, and $54.0 million for the third quarter of fiscal 1998. Net product revenue for the fourth quarter was $45.7 million, compared to $33.9 million in the fourth quarter last year, and $44.8 million for the immediately preceding quarter.

Service revenue for the fourth quarter of $9.8 million represented a 19% increase over the $8.2 million reported in the fourth quarter of the previous year, and a 6% increase over the $9.2 million reported in the immediately preceding quarter.

Net income for fiscal 1998 was $18.0 million, or 62 cents diluted earnings per share, compared to $5.7 million, or 22 cents diluted earnings per share, for the previous fiscal year. Total revenue for fiscal 1998 rose 30% to $200.0 million, as compared to $153.7 million for fiscal 1997. Net product revenue for fiscal 1998 was $163.7 million, an increase of 36% over fiscal 1997?s net product revenues of $120.4 million. Service revenue for fiscal 1998 was $36.3 million, up 9% over the prior year.

The gross profit margin for the fourth quarter was 35.9%, compared to 34.5% for the same period last year, and 34.5% for the third quarter of fiscal 1998. The gross profit margin for fiscal 1998 was 35.5%, compared to 32.5% for fiscal 1997.

IN THE QUARTER ENDED JANUARY 3, 1998, THE COMPANY ADOPTED STATEMENT OF FINANCIAL ACCOUNTING STANDARDS NO. 128, "EARNINGS PER SHARE." ALL PRIOR PERIODS HAVE BEEN RESTATED ACCORDINGLY.

Revenue Trend:

The Company completed the fourth quarter with approximately 96% of product revenue derived from the sale of products for the Open Systems environment, compared to approximately 81% and 94% for the fourth quarter of last year and the third quarter of fiscal 1998, respectively.

"I am very pleased with the Company?s performance for fiscal 1998. In particular, we achieved one of our primary benchmark goals of $200 million of top line revenue," said Earl Pearlman, MTI president and chief executive officer. "During the first half of fiscal 1998 we concentrated on the expansion and enhancement of our products lines, including the release of our flagship product, the Gladiator 3200. We also announced the Gladiator ESS line, which provided our customers with a true terabyte-class of high performance storage solutions. In the second quarter of fiscal 1998, the Company launched the Gladiator 6000 storage system, a new product family of truly fault-tolerant storage solutions utilizing the Company?s proprietary controller technology.

During the second half of fiscal 1998, the Company increased its investment in its ability to provide its customers with world-class service and support. The capstone of these efforts was the February 1998 opening of the Company?s Customer Response Center.

As we look forward to fiscal 1999, I am very excited by the opportunities presented by what we believe is a strong and growing storage market. During the coming year we will focus on exploring our opportunities for entry into the Pacific Rim market place, as well as seeking strategic partnerships within the OEM channel. In addition, the Company plans to begin shipment of its full line of fibre channel products for both the Gladiator 3000 and 6000 product families. And as always, we will continue to seek ways to improve our ability to provide the highest level of service and support to our customers," said Mr. Pearlman.

Expense Overview:

Selling, general and administrative expenses for the fourth quarter were $11.1 million, compared to $9.6 million for the fourth quarter last year, and $10.5 million for the immediately preceding quarter. The increase of $1.5 million over the same quarter of the previous year was primarily the result of an increase in compensation-related sales costs due to increased staff and higher revenues. Selling, general and administrative expenses for fiscal 1998 were $41.1 million, as compared to $34.9 million for the prior fiscal year.

Research and development expenses for the fourth quarter of fiscal 1998 were $3.5 million, compared to $3.0 million in the same quarter last year and $3.1 million in the immediately prior quarter. The increase in expenses over the same quarter of the previous year was primarily due to increases in project development costs. Research and development expenses for fiscal 1998 were $12.5 million, as compared to $10.1 million for fiscal 1997.

Balance Sheet Summary:

Cash on hand at the end of fiscal 1998 was $7.8 million, compared to $3.5 million at the end of the prior fiscal year. Trade accounts receivable increased $12.4 million from the end of the prior fiscal year, primarily as the result of an increase in sales. Inventories increased $4.2 million, or approximately 29% from the end of fiscal 1997, primarily as the result of an increase in trade inventories held in both the US and Europe. Total bank debt and notes payable declined $16.1 million from the end of fiscal 1997, primarily as a result of the reduction in the outstanding portion of the Company?s bank line of credit. Trade payables and accrued liabilities increased $8.5 million from the end of fiscal 1997, primarily as a result of increased accounts payable related to the increase in inventories.

MTI?s mission is to be the premier provider of high-performance, cross-platform data storage management solutions for the enterprise. MTI integrates distributed data management software, a hierarchy of modular storage systems and on-site services to meet the mass storage needs of its customers.
