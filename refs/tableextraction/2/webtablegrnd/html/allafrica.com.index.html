Coffee Confab Hears Radical Proposal to Boost Prices

At a London hotel where a cup of coffee costs nearly a dozen times what farmers receive for a 100-pound bag of coffee beans, the World Coffee Conference is convening this weekend. With prices at a 30-year low, and African growers suffering sharp income declines, some blame World Bank policies for global overproduction. The development group Oxfam is proposing burning millions of excess bags to increase producer revenues.
