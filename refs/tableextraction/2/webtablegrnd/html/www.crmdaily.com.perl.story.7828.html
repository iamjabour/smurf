Ariba Tugs at Value Chain Management

With chairman and CEO Keith Krach at the helm, Ariba aims to help companies optimize their business processes by streamlining interactions with trading partners.

Ariba, Inc. (Nasdaq: ARBA) has stepped into the emerging value chain management (VCM) arena with a series of new product and partnership announcements.

The company introduced its Ariba VCM Platform, which extends its existing B2B commerce platform to enable real-time collaboration and transactions and to offer users a choice between standards-based integration and out-of-the-box functionality. In addition, Ariba has extended its Commerce Services Network to support a broader range of direct materials transactions.

Ariba also announced partnerships with Syncra Systems for collaborative planning and replenishment; SeeCommerce for value chain performance management; and Zeborg for operations value chain spending analytics. The company said these partnerships will help it enter the VCM space.

VCM Va-Va-Va-Voom

VCM helps companies focus on optimizing their business by streamlining interactions with trading partners and procurement processes. Ariba said that without VCM, such interactions often are manual, expensive and poorly integrated.

According to industry analysts, VCM applications are expected to represent a software license market of US$21 billion by 2003 and $42 billion by 2005. Ariba said this dramatic increase will occur because current ERP and supply chain management software is not focused on improving value chain interactions -- but new applications will have that focus.

Ariba said its new VCM products are different from -- yet complement -- existing ERP and supply chain management applications.

Fancy Footwork

Earlier this year, Ariba announced the pending acquisition of Agile Software Corporation (Nasdaq: AGIL). The company's moves -- encompassing everything from new products to partnerships and acquisitions -- are part of a strategy that Ariba said will bring increased return on investment (ROI) to its customers.

The company added that it plans to extend its VCM strategy in the future by introducing event-based process coordination, replenishment signals, advanced ship notice and blanket orders.
