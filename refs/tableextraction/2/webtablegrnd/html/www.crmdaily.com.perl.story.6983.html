Airline To Test In-Flight Web Service

Tenzing Communications, Inc. announced Wednesday an agreement with Scandinavian Airlines System (SAS) to start on-board testing of Tenzing's in-flight communications services.

The companies, in conjunction with European wireless access provider Telia Mobile , will provide transparent service via Telia's wireless roaming network and Tenzing's terrestrial Internet service provider (ISP) to in-flight and grounded passengers.

Sky Roaming

During the trial, which is scheduled to take place later this year, passengers will be able to send and receive e-mail and access on-board cached Web pages, including airline and travel portals, during flight.

Users will be able to use e-mail and Internet services before, during and after their travel, the companies said. In addition, passengers will be able to "roam" transparently across services on the wireless and terrestrial networks while still using their local service provider.

SAS said it is the first European-based airline to announce testing of in-flight e-mail and Internet service for passengers. The company added that it plans to find ways to offer business passengers access to corporate e-mail accounts despite the presence of firewalls set up to deny access.

Access to the airborne system will be provided through an in-cabin wireless local area network (WLAN) using IEEE 802.11b technology to connect passengers to the Tenzing system.

Expanding Service

Jan Olson, vice president of product and service development at SAS, said if the test program proves successful, the wireless service will be expanded to all of the airline's lounges worldwide.

Telia Mobile said it already has deployed wireless services in airports and other public areas that let passengers access a suite of services designed for travelers. A roaming agreement will allow Tenzing Global subscribers and Telia Mobile's HomeRun subscribers to use either company's network services.

The Tenzing system also is being tested on Air Canada flights, with negotiations underway for system implementation on other U.S. and international carriers. Tenzing said it will begin full deployment of the system on Cathay Pacific Airways in the first quarter of this year.

Future plans call for Tenzing to implement broadband Internet access using advanced satellite communications technology for airline passengers, the company said.
