About

the Internet Weather Report TM

The IWR TM presents ongoing animated scans of conditions within the Internet. It is like daily newspaper or television weather radar reports, except instead of being about real world meteorology, it is about conditions inside the Internet itself. The IWR is presented in geographical maps that show lag, which is round trip time ( latency ) from our offices in Austin, Texas to thousands of Internet domains worldwide, currently every four hours, six times a day, seven days a week, using ICMP ECHO (ping).
How do the sizes of the circles indicate latency? How do the colors of the circles indicate numbers of domains? How do the circles change with time?
IWR reports are Java applets, for nonstop viewing and single-stepping frame by frame. Sit back and watch the weather, or examine each scan. Problems with Java?
