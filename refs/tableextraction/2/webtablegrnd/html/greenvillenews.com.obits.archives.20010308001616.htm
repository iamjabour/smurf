Gary R. Brewer, San Antonio, Texas

Gary Ridgeway Brewer, AAF, AIFD, PFCI, of San Antonio, Texas, formerly of Greenville, passed away Monday March 5, 2001, at the age of 55.



Gary was founder of A Touch of Class Flowers & Gifts and Just Weddings and Floral Events by Gary Brewer in Greenville. He has been actively involved in the floral industry for many years. He is a member of the American Institute of Floral Designers, the American Academy of Floriculture, Professional Floral Commentators International and the Society of American Florists. In his role as an AFS Education Services Team member, Gary developed and presented a program entitled, "All About Weddings" and another "All About Tropicals", which was created for the Hawaiian Department of Agriculture. He was designed or commentated at hundreds of shows during his career, and he is especially well known for his signature, opulent garden style design. Gary has served six terms as president of Upper Piedmont Florists' Association. There is no facet of floral industry that Gary has not touched. He has designed for presidential inaugurations, the Academy Awards, Charisma Floats and the Tournament of Roses parades. For some, however, he is best remembered for having a dahlia, "The Gary B", named in his honor by the American Dahlia Society. "Designer of the year" is a title that has been bestowed on Gary many times. During his career Gary has held this title for Arkansas, South Carolina, Texas, Southeast Texas, Upper Piedmont and the Ozarks. He has also been named Southern Retail Designer of the Year and the American Japanese Invitational Designer of the Year. He received the People's Choice Award in the 1991 Sylvia Cup Competition at SAF. Gary has also done work to help other floral designers. He has designed for two floral selections guides for Redbook Florist Services, as well as developing their Master Consultant Program. Among Gary's lecture credits is the National Federation of Garden Clubs. Gary was currently the Director of Product Development for Garden Ridge Manufacturing of San Antonio, Texas.



He was preceded in death by his father, William S. Brewer; grandparents, Gary and Lottie Black Ridgeway and Robert R. and Mattie Thompson Brewer; and his beloved Sandy.



Survivors include his mother, Catherine Ridgeway Brewer, of Belton; a brother, Bill Brewer and wife, Pat, of Raleigh, N.C.; a sister, Pat Capell and husband, Ken, of Anderson; a niece, Amy Gagner and husband, Chris, of Wake Forest, N.C.; a great-niece, Sarah Grace; a partner in life of 20 years, Ricky Henry of San Antonio, Texas; his loving pets, Oscar, Lucky and Polly; and many special friends.



Gary touched many lives. We will miss his smile, laughter, and funny stories. He was loved by all who knew him.



The service to celebrate Gary's life will be held Friday at 7 p.m. at the Sullivan-King Mortuary, Northeast Chapel, with the Rev. Ray Moore and the Rev. Phil Dixon officiating, and visitation to follow until 8:30 p.m. Gary will be shipped to Little Rock, Ark., where the visitation will be held Saturday from 7 to 8:30 p.m., with funeral services Sunday at 2 p.m. at Roller-Chenal Funeral Home, 13801 Chenal Parkway, (501)224-8300. Burial will be held at Crestlawn Memorial Park in Conway, Ark. Flowers are requested, or if desired, memorials may be made to: Gary Brewer Scholarship Fund, P.O. Box 500, Plummerville, Ark. 72127.

Mrs. V.H. Gillespie, Greenville

Elsie Burnett Gillespie, 101, of 216 Sandpiper Way, died at her home Tuesday, March 6, 2001.



She was the widow of Victor Harris Gillespie. Born in Spartanburg Oct. 18, 1889, she was the daughter of the late William Perry and Arabelle Clippard Burnett.



Mr. and Mrs. Gillespie joined First Baptist Church, Greenville, in 1929, and were active members there and as charter members of Downtown Baptist Church. She was still an active member of Crescent Community Club and had long been a member of Behethland Butler Chapter of the DAR.



Before her marriage, she was the first female field representative of the Sunday School Board of the South Carolina Baptist Convention, traveling the state to promote Sunday School programs and materials in churches. After her marriage and removal to Greenville, she was a homemaker and an active volunteer in church and community. An important interest was as a member of the Auxiliary to the Greenacre Home for Girls. During depression years and before social services, families were sometimes forced to place their children in homes to be cared for temporarily. She was a tireless advocate and fundraiser for those girls. Later, she was an Auxiliary member at the formation of the Sara Simpson Gossett Home and personally decorated and equipped the Home.



After 10 years as a full-time grandmother, she became interested in sales and teaching sewing and handwork at Pleasantburg Fabrics. At age 75, she changed careers again and became a salesperson at Patterson's at Lewis Plaza, where she finally retired 10 years later.



She is survived by a daughter and son-in-law, Sarah and Basil Manly; and by four grandchildren and their spouses, Sarah and Mark Cornish of Chester Springs, Pa., Basil Manly of Greenville, Jean and Michael McDowell of Charlotte, N.C., and Mary and Jim Mounce of Greenville. There are five great-grandchildren, Sarah C. Manly of Atlanta, Lindsay and Meredith McDowell of Charlotte, Katie Cornish of Chester Springs, and Taylor Mounce of Greenville.



Her sister, Dorothy (Mrs. Walter) Betsill of Greenville, also survives in addition to numerous beloved cousins and nieces and nephews. She was predeceased by one sister, Sarah (Mrs. Paul) Gillespie; and three brothers, Bertram, Frederick, and Walter Burnett, all of Spartanburg. Also surviving is her trusted companion of 14 years, Minnie Coleman.



Services will be held Friday, March 9, at 11 a.m. in the Chapel of The Mackey Mortuary, Century Drive. Visitation will be held from 10 to 11 a.m. Interment will be held at Woodlawn Memorial Park.



In lieu of flowers, memorials may be made to Downtown Baptist Church, 101 W. McBee Ave., Greenville, SC 29601.



The family will be at 2 Chanticleer Drive.
