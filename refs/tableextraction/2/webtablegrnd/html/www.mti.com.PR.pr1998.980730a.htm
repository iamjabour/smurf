MTI Reports First Quarter Fiscal 1999 Results

MTI's Net Income up 32% over Prior Year's First Quarter

ANAHEIM, Calif., July 30, 1998 -- MTI Technology Corp. (Nasdaq:MTIC) today reported net income for the first quarter of fiscal 1999 ended July 4, 1998 of $4.1 million, or 14 cents diluted earnings per share, compared to net income of $3.1 million, or 11 cents diluted earnings per share, in the same quarter of the previous year, and $5.8 million, or 19 cents diluted earnings per share for the immediately preceding quarter. Total revenue for the first quarter rose 13% to $49.4 million, compared to $43.8 million for last year?s first quarter, but was down 11% compared to $55.5 million for the fourth quarter of fiscal 1998. Net product revenue for the first quarter was $39.2 million, compared to $35.3 million in the first quarter last year, and $45.7 million for the immediately preceding quarter.

Service revenue for the first quarter of $10.2 million represented a 20% increase over the $8.5 million reported in the first quarter of the previous year, and a 4% increase over the $9.8 million reported in the immediately preceding quarter.

The gross profit margin for the first quarter was 36.2%, compared to 36.3% for the same period last year, and 35.9% for the fourth quarter of fiscal 1998.

IN THE QUARTER ENDED JANUARY 3, 1998, THE COMPANY ADOPTED STATEMENT OF FINANCAIL ACCOUNTING STANDARDS NO. 128, "EARNINGS PER SHARE." ALL PRIOR PERIODS HAVE BEEN RESTATED ACCORDINGLY.

Business Trends

"The first quarter of fiscal 1999 presented both challenges and opportunities for MTI," said Earl Pearlman, MTI president and chief executive officer. "Although overall top line revenues grew year-over-year, total revenues for the first quarter were lower than anticipated. This shortfall was due to lower than expected product revenues from our domestic markets primarily related to the delay in the availability of our fibre channel products, and the impact of additional sales staffing requirements in certain geographical regions. From a positive standpoint, product sales from our European operations were up 24% and 22% from the same quarter last year and the immediately preceding quarter, respectively, indicating a significant improvement over the fourth quarter of fiscal 1998. Additionally, revenues from our customer service operation were at an all time high, with increases in both our European operations and domestically. I am also very pleased with our gross margin level, which was above 36% for only the second time in the past nine quarters," said Pearlman.

"Clearly, to be able to attain our revenue growth targets for the remainder of this fiscal year is heavily dependent on our ability to bring to market our fibre channel RAID products. To this end, we have shifted additional resources within our research and development groups to focus solely on completing the initial models of our Gladiator 3600 Fibre Channel RAID array, which we anticipate will begin shipping during the month of August. Reflecting our commitment to the expansion of our sales organization, we have hired a net seven additional salespersons domestically since the beginning of the first quarter, and a net five additional salespersons in our European operations. As I have communicated in previous quarters, we strongly believe that the Open Systems storage market presents substantial opportunities for MTI and that we will best be able to pursue these opportunities by maintaining our focus on the needs and requirements of our customers," stated Mr. Pearlman.

Expense Overview

Selling, general and administrative expenses for the first quarter were $10.6 million, compared to $9.7 million for the first quarter last year, and $11.1 million for the immediately preceding quarter.

The increase of $0.9 million over the same quarter of the previous year was primarily the result of an increase in compensation-related sales costs due to increased staff and higher revenues.

Research and development expenses for the first quarter were $3.5 million, compared to $3.0 million in the same quarter last year and $3.5 million in the immediately prior quarter. The increase in expenses year-to-year was primarily due to increases in project development costs and increased staff costs.

Balance Sheet Summary

Cash on hand at the end of the first quarter of fiscal 1999 was $3.8 million, compared to $7.8 million at the end of the prior fiscal year. Trade accounts receivable increased $2.8 million from the end of the prior fiscal year, primarily as the result of an increase in sales in the Company?s European operations. Inventories decreased $2.1 million, or approximately 11% from the end of fiscal 1998, primarily as the result of a decrease in trade inventories held in the domestic operations. Total bank debt declined $1.8 million while trade payables and accrued liabilities decreased $3.1 million from the end of fiscal 1998.

MTI?s mission is to be the premier provider of high-performance, cross-platform data storage management solutions for the enterprise. MTI integrates distributed data management software, a hierarchy of modular storage systems and on-site services to meet the mass storage needs of its customers.
