TABLE OF STATE MCLE REQUIREMENTS

For your convenience ALI-ABA has compiled the following table of the 40 states that have adopted mandatory continuing legal education (MCLE). For further information on your state's MCLE requirements, please contact your MCLE Board or Commission .

State Requirements
Alabama 12 hours per calendar year. Reporting date: December 31.
Arizona 15 hours per year including 3 hours ethics. Reporting date: September 15.
Arkansas 12 hours per year including 1 hour ethics. Reporting date: June 30.
California 25 hours per 3-year period including 4 hours legal ethics, 1 hour substance abuse/emotional distress, and 1 hour elimination of bias. Reporting date: January 31.
Colorado 45 hours per 3-year period including 7 hours ethics. Reporting date: anytime within 3 year period.
Delaware 24 hours per 2-year period including 4 hrs. ethics. Reporting date: December 31 every 2nd year.
Florida 30 hours per 3-year period including 5 hours ethics. Reporting date: assigned month every 3 years
Georgia 12 hours per year including 1 hour ethics, 1 hour professionalism. Reporting date: January 31.
Idaho 30 hours per 3-year period including 2 hrs. legal ethics and/or professionalism. Reporting date: January 10 every 3rd year depending on date of admission.
Indiana 36 hours per 3-year period with 6 hour minimum per year including 3 hours ethics. Reporting date: 30 days after program (sponsor must report).
Iowa 15 hours per calendar year including 2 hours ethics every 2 years. Reporting date: March 1.
Kansas 12 hours per year including 2 hours ethics. Reporting date: July 31.
Kentucky 12.5 hours per year including 2 hours ethics. Reporting date: June 30.
Louisiana 15 hours per year including 1 hour ethics/professional responsibility. Reporting date: January 31.
Maine 11 hours per year including one hour ethics. Reporting date: at time of annual registration.
Minnesota 45 hours per 3-year period including 3 hours ethics and 2 hours elimination of bias. Reporting date: August 30.
Mississippi 12 hours per year including 1 hour ethics/professional responsibility. Reporting date: July 31.
Missouri 15 hours per year including 3 hours ethics every 3 years. Reporting date July 31.
Montana 15 hours per year. Reporting date: March 1.
Nevada 12 hours per year including 2 hours ethics. Reporting date: March 1.
New Hampshire 12 hours per year including 2 hours ethics/professional responsibility. Reporting date: August 1.
New Mexico 15 hours per year including 1 hour ethics / professional responsibility. Reporting date: April 1.
New York 24 hours per 2-year period including 4 hours of ethics; new admittees 32 hours per 2-year period including 3 hours ethics / professionalism, 6 hours skills, 7 hours practice management or substantive areas of practice. Reporting date: at time of biennial registration.
No. Carolina 12 hours per year including 2 hours ethics/professional responsibility or professional liability; special 3-hour block ethics requirement once every 3 years. Reporting date: February 28.
No. Dakota 45 hours per 3 year period including 3 hours of ethics. Reporting date: July 30
Ohio 24 hours per 2 years including 2.5 hours ethics and substance abuse of which 30 minutes must be on substance abuse, 60 minutes on the Code of Professional Responsibility, and 60 minutes on professionalism. Reporting date: every 2 years by January 31
Oklahoma 12 hours per year including 1 hour ethics. Reporting date: February 15.
Oregon 45 hours per 3-year period including 6 hours ethics . Reporting date: every 3rd year depending on year of admission, except initial one-year period for new admittees and reinstated members.
Pennsylvania 12 hours per year including 1 hour ethics. Reporting date: 30 days after program (sponsor must report).
Rhode Island 10 hours per year including 2 hours ethics. Reporting date: June 30.
So. Carolina 14 hours per year including 2 hours ethics. Reporting date: January 15.
Tennessee 15 hours per year including 3 hours ethics. Reporting date March 1.
Texas 15 hours per year including 3 hours ethics / professional responsibility. Reporting date: last day of birth month each year.
Utah 24 hours per 2-year period plus 3 hours ethics. Reporting date: every 2 years by December 31.
Vermont 20 hours per 2-year period including 2 hours of ethics. Reporting date: every 2 years by July 2.
Virginia 12 hours per year including 2 hours ethics. Reporting date: June 30.
Washington 45 hours over 3 years including 6 ethics hours. Reporting date: January 31 every 3rd year.
West Virginia 24 hours per 2-year period including 3 hours ethics or office management. Reporting date: June 30 every 2 years.
Wisconsin 30 hours per 2-year period including 3 hours ethics. Reporting date: February 1 every 2nd year.
Wyoming 15 hours per year. Reporting date: January 31.
