Proxim, Inc. Reports Q4 and Year End 1999 Results

SUNNYVALE, Calif., January 25, 2000

? Proxim, Inc. (NASDAQ: PROX) announced today its financial results for the fourth quarter and fiscal year ended December 31, 1999. Revenue for the fourth quarter of 1999 was $20,313,000 compared to revenue of $14,659,000 for the fourth quarter of 1998. Revenue for the year ended December 31, 1999 was $69,067,000, compared to revenue of $49,711,000 for the year ended December 31, 1998.

During the fourth quarter of 1999, the company acquired Wavespan Corporation, a developer of wireless broadband access solutions. The acquisition was accounted for as a purchase transaction, and during the fourth quarter of 1999, the company took a charge of $5,883,000 for purchased in-process research and development, and amortization of goodwill and intangibles of $297,000. During the fourth quarter of 1998, the company took a $1,000,000 charge to selling, general and administrative expense related to an investment in a startup wireless services company.

Excluding the charges, pro forma net income for the fourth quarter of 1999 was $2,862,000, or $.21 per share (diluted), compared to pro forma net income for the fourth quarter of 1998 of $2,007,000, or $.17 per share (diluted). Including the charges, the net loss for the fourth quarter of 1999 was $(1,186,000), or $(0.09) per share (diluted), and net income for the fourth quarter of 1998 was $1,007,000, or $0.09 per share (diluted).

Excluding the charges for purchased in-process research and development and the amortization of goodwill and intangibles, pro forma net income for the year ended December 31, 1999 was $9,166,000, or $.72 per share (diluted), compared to pro forma net income for 1998 of $5,698,000, or $.51 per share (diluted), excluding the charge for the investment in a startup wireless services company. Including all charges, net income for the year ended December 31, 1999 was $3,118,000, or $.25 per share (diluted), compared to net income for 1998 of $4,698,000, or $.42 per share (diluted).

"During Q4 1999, Proxim achieved record revenues and earnings (net of acquisition-related charges) based on solid contributions from both our RangeLAN2 commercial products and Symphony consumer products," said David C. King, Proxim chairman, president and CEO. "More importantly, Proxim has broadened its technology portfolio and expanded its product offerings with the acquisitions of Wavespan Corporation, completed in December 1999, and Micrilor, Inc., announced separately today. With these acquisitions, we look forward to capitalizing on our leadership position in the commercial wireless LAN and home networking markets, and pursuing future growth opportunities in the corporate and ISP marketplace."

About Proxim

Headquartered in Sunnyvale, Calif., Proxim, Inc., has more than 15 years of experience in the wireless LAN market and is the world's leading supplier of spread spectrum wireless LAN products to OEMs and wireless solutions providers. In 1994, Proxim was first to market with its industry-leading RangeLAN2™ 2.4 GHz frequency hopping wireless LAN product family, which has attracted more than 100 OEMs and wireless solutions providers worldwide. In 1998, the company introduced its high-performance RangeLAN802™ product line, which operates at 2 Mbps and is fully compliant with the IEEE 802.11 standard. In 1999, Proxim established itself as a leader in the emerging home networking market with its award-winning Symphony™ suite of cordless networking products for home and small office environments.

Proxim is a founding member of the Wireless LAN Interoperability Forum (WLI Forum), established to deliver and test interoperable wireless LAN products and services ( www.wlif.com ). Proxim is also a core member of the Home Radio Frequency Working Group (HomeRF), which is committed to providing a broad range of interoperable cordless consumer devices ( www.homerf.org ). See www.proxim.com for additional information.
