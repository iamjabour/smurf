Educational Opportunities with

Continuing Education Credits

The Society provides a large number of workshops, seminars and satellite broadcasts on timely property and casualty insurance issues. Visit the Learning Center to learn more about educational opportunities.

Visibility

This is the key to your prestige in the industry. The Society's public relations program , with its Insurance Guide for the Media , national advertising campaign, and national speaker's bureau , is focused on making CPCU recognized as the mark of ultimate professionalism in the insurance industry. Members gain professional visibility by building their reputations in the industry through Society service , by teaching, conducting seminars, leading chapter meetings, and participating in chapter projects.

Networking

Your local chapter offers opportunities to network with CPCUs in your area. Special Interest Sections offer opportunities to network with professionals in your area of specialization. The Society's annual meetings provide opportunities to network with CPCUs, new designees and guests from across the nation.

Leadership Opportunities

The CPCU Society is volunteer-driven. It is organized by and for CPCUs. Leadership opportunities at the chapter and national level offer members the chance to hone their knowledge, leadership skills and experience.

Continuing Professional Development

CPCU Society members are eligible to earn CPD certification through a voluntary incentive program that recognizes achievement in continuing education and professional activities.

Special Interest Sections

The CPCU Society has 14 Special Interest Sections to accommodate Society members with different interests and views. Each section publishes its own quarterly newsletter, sponsors symposia, and conducts research projects. Members can belong to as many sections as they choose.
